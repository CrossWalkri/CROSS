## Purpose

This document records organizations and frameworks that have published material, but that material does not fit Part XII compatibility coverage for specific structural reasons. The Partial Coverage Map is distinct from the Gap Map (which records true null findings, where no published framework exists) and from Part XII compatibility statements (which record full coverage). The distinction matters because lumping partials in with true nulls misrepresents organizations that have made deliberate choices, published in adjacent scopes, or operate by design without measurement frameworks.

A formal CROSS+WALKRI Part XII compatibility statement requires that a published framework: (a) is publicly accessible at a stable URL, (b) specifies requirements that grant programs or grantees must meet, and (c) is a regulatory instrument, formal standard with declared decision-standing rules and named maintainers, or widely-adopted sector-reference framework. Entries below fail one or more of these conditions in a way that is structural, not accidental.

An entry is partial rather than null when the published material exists but does not satisfy at least one of: Gate Type specification, Obligation Mode declaration, Public Benefit Mechanism declaration, or Access Condition specification at a level sufficient for the Part XII threshold conditions. Where the published material satisfies these conditions but the framework's scope falls outside grantee outcome measurement (peer review methodology, professional ethics, charity ratings, government accountability), the entry sits under Adjacent Frameworks rather than as a true partial.

The five categories below organize partials by the reason they fall outside Part XII rather than by sector or geography.

## Adjacent Frameworks

Frameworks that are formally published and rigorous in their own right, but operate in a scope adjacent to grantee outcome measurement (peer review, professional ethics, charity ratings, government accountability). These frameworks may be useful reference standards for CROSS+WALKRI implementers, but they do not themselves specify the Gate Type, Obligation Mode, Public Benefit Mechanism, and Access Condition fields that a grantee outcome measurement framework declares.

## Intentional Non-Adoption

Organizations that have deliberately chosen not to publish a grantee measurement framework. The choice is itself a structural position, often grounded in grantmaking architectures that decline written outcome specification and rest verification on named relational conditions (continuity of relationship, reputational continuity, named historical connection), in decentralized strategy, or in principled rejection of measurement architecture. These are not gaps in the ecosystem; they are considered design decisions.

| Organization | What Was Found | Why Not a Part XII Standard |
|:--|:--|:--|
| MacKenzie Scott / Yield Giving | Deliberately no application process; trust-based unrestricted giving; published commitment to minimize reporting requirements | No measurement framework applicable by design: recipients self-determine use, and the absence of measurement architecture is itself a published position |
| Open Philanthropy | Explicitly rejects formalized measurement in most programs; light-touch check-ins only; methodology pages describe the rejection | Deliberate non-adoption; the rejection is publicly stated rather than hidden, but no grantee MEL framework results |
| Open Society Foundations | No unified evaluation policy; program-by-program approaches; deliberately decentralized strategy; Economic Justice Program MEL sub-strategy (2021-24) is the only program-level published exception | Deliberately decentralized; no foundation-wide grantee framework published; the EJP exception is one program portfolio only |
| Schmidt Futures | Primarily proactive grantmaking; no public-facing framework document | Deliberately proactive-only by design; no open application evaluation standard |

## Relationship to the Gap Map

The Gap Map at `statements/meta/CROSS-WALKRI-coverage-gap-map-0_1_0.md` records organizations and frameworks that conduct evaluation work but have not published any framework meeting the Part XII threshold. The two documents are complementary: an absence on the published side (Gap Map) is structurally different from a presence in an adjacent scope (Partial Coverage Map, Adjacent Frameworks), a deliberate non-publishing posture (Partial Coverage Map, Intentional Non-Adoption), or a closed program (Partial Coverage Map, Historical and Inactive).

Future revisions of either document should treat the categorization as a live decision: organizations move between Gap Map, Partial Coverage Map, and Part XII as their published material evolves. The changelog of each document should reflect such migrations.
