# CROSS+WALKRI Coverage Gap Map

## Frameworks and Traditions Searched Without Formal Published Standards

---

## Purpose

This document records every major giving tradition, foundation, bilateral agency, philanthropic body, and grant evaluation framework that was researched during the development of CROSS+WALKRI compatibility documentation and for which no formally published, linkable standard was found.

A formally published standard, for the purposes of CROSS+WALKRI compatibility, means a document that: is publicly accessible at a stable URL, specifies requirements that grant programs or grantees must meet, and is either a regulatory instrument, a formal standard with a governance process, or a widely adopted sector-reference framework. Practice descriptions, job postings, internal policy summaries, annual reports, and eligibility criteria alone do not qualify.

Null findings are documented here for three reasons. First, they demonstrate that CROSS+WALKRI's compatibility claims rest on genuine research and that the absence of coverage reflects the absence of published material rather than incomplete search. Second, they map real gaps in the grants ecosystem's standards infrastructure: the traditions and organizations listed here represent contexts where grantees, reviewers, operators, and funders operate without a shared measurement framework. Third, the pattern of absences reinforces why a universal standard is needed: CROSS+WALKRI was designed to provide the measurement architecture that these traditions lack at the specification level.

## What the Gap Map Shows

The pattern across these null findings reveals three distinct types of absence:

**Type 1: Measurement happens but is not codified.** Many major foundations and bilateral agencies conduct serious, high-quality evaluation work but have not published that work as a standard others can adopt. Robert Bosch Stiftung, King Baudouin Foundation, and Leverhulme Trust are examples. In these cases, CROSS+WALKRI provides a published architecture that these foundations' evaluation practices already implicitly implement, and that grantees seeking to satisfy their unstated expectations can use as a conformant baseline.

**Type 2: Cultural giving traditions without administrative infrastructure.** Mécénat, mäzenatentum, ubuntu-based giving, and religious giving traditions predate formal measurement frameworks and operate without them by design. CROSS+WALKRI does not seek to impose measurement on traditions that have not requested it; the gap map records that these traditions exist and that no incompatible published standard was found.

**Type 3: Sector infrastructure gaps where no standard should exist but does not.** The absence of a pan-African philanthropic measurement standard, a unified Latin American grant evaluation framework, or a published standard for historical patronage traditions is a genuine gap in the ecosystem. The global grants sector lacks standards for several significant geographic and cultural contexts. CROSS+WALKRI's format-agnostic, culturally neutral architecture is designed to be adoptable in these contexts precisely because it does not encode any particular cultural giving tradition.

## Implications for Future Compatibility Work

Where a publishing body listed here subsequently publishes a formal standard, a compatibility statement should be written and this entry updated or removed. The gap map is a living document.

For organizations listed as "practice norm only," CROSS+WALKRI compatibility claims in those contexts should be framed as: "A CROSS+WALKRI-conformant program satisfies the unstated measurement expectations of [organization] by providing structured documentation of what the program produces, what evidence supports that claim, and how the causal pathway is declared."
