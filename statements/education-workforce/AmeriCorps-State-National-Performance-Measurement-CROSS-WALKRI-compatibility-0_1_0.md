# AmeriCorps State and National Performance Measurement

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

AmeriCorps State and National grants, governed by 45 CFR Part 2522, operate under a performance measurement architecture that combines mandatory logic model development, a nationally prescribed output/outcome pairing menu, and a tiered independent evaluation obligation triggered by funding threshold. This architecture is structurally distinct from the general uniform guidance at 2 CFR 200 and from the open-ended performance measure designs permitted in many other federal programs. CROSS+WALKRI satisfies the Part 2522 performance measurement requirements structurally: the nationally prescribed output/outcome pairing menu is the regulatory form of CROSS's entry specification gate with a nationally pre-defined indicator set, and WALKRI's operational definition and evidence form requirements address the specification gap the national menu creates at the program level, where grantees must translate nationally defined pairings into local intake instruments without further specification guidance.

---

## The AmeriCorps State and National Performance Measurement Framework

The AmeriCorps State and National performance measurement framework has four distinctive structural elements that separate it from the general federal grants evaluation architecture.

**Mandatory logic model at application:** 45 CFR Part 2522 requires every applicant to develop and submit a logic model as a condition of application. The logic model must specify inputs, activities, outputs, and outcomes in a structured format before any award is made. This pre-award logic model requirement corresponds to CROSS's entry specification gate operating at the application stage: the Theory of Change hierarchy (activities, outputs, outcomes, goals) must be declared before any member is enrolled. AmeriCorps does not accept applications that omit the logic model; it is a gate, not a recommendation.

**Nationally prescribed output/outcome pairing menu:** Unlike most federal programs that allow grantees to design their own performance measures, AmeriCorps publishes an Annual Performance Measures Instructions document specifying allowable output/outcome pairings. Grantees select from this menu rather than freely designing their measurement architecture. Each pairing specifies which output measure must accompany which outcome measure, preventing grantees from selecting an output without committing to the corresponding outcome. This pairing structure is the regulatory form of CROSS's obligation architecture: selecting a Build obligation (output) automatically requires a matching Change obligation (outcome), and these are pre-specified nationally rather than designed locally.

**Tiered independent evaluation obligation:** 45 CFR 2522.710 creates a graduated evaluation requirement. Programs below $500,000 in average annual grant funding must conduct internal performance reporting against the prescribed measures. Programs at or above $500,000 must arrange an independent evaluation, conducted by an evaluator without a conflict of interest, and must submit the results with their competitive reapplication. This tiered structure corresponds to CROSS's independent attestation pathway: above a defined funding threshold, the attestation cannot come from the program itself and must be independently produced.

**Multi-year output-then-outcome reporting sequence:** AmeriCorps requires grantees to report outputs at the end of year one, then outputs plus intermediate outcomes at years two and three of a grant cycle. This phased evidence sequence corresponds to CROSS's completion gate structure: the program does not claim outcome-level results in year one, but documents the output foundation on which the outcome evidence will be built in subsequent years. The distinction matters for CROSS's causality stance field: a year-one program reporting only outputs should declare a Build obligation, not a Change obligation, for year-one evidence.

## How CROSS Satisfies the Part 2522 Framework

CROSS addresses the Part 2522 evaluation requirements at the structural level.

**Entry specification gate and logic model requirement:** The mandatory pre-award logic model requirement is the regulatory form of CROSS's entry specification gate. CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy (activities, outputs, outcomes, goals) maps directly to the AmeriCorps logic model structure. A CROSS-conformant program completes the logic model's structural requirements as a consequence of entry gate conformance, since CROSS requires each element of the hierarchy to be declared with specificity before any application is accepted. CROSS's eleven-field indicator specification applied to the logic model's output and outcome nodes produces entries that satisfy Part 2522's logic model requirements and provide the measurement precision the regulation requires.

**Prescribed output/outcome pairings and CROSS obligation types:** The AmeriCorps national output/outcome pairing menu corresponds to CROSS's Build and Change obligation architecture. Each menu pairing specifies a Build obligation (countable output) and the Change obligation (outcome) that must accompany it. Programs cannot select an output without committing to the corresponding outcome, which is the structural principle of CROSS's obligation pairing: a Build obligation alone is permissible only when the program is explicitly not claiming outcome-level change. The national menu prevents this gap by requiring paired commitment at application.

**Tiered evaluation and CROSS attestation pathways:** The $500,000 threshold for independent evaluation corresponds to CROSS's independent attestation gate triggered at scale. Below threshold, the program's own annual reporting constitutes the attestation. Above threshold, independent evaluation results submitted with reapplication constitute an independent attestation corpus. CROSS's attestation gate requirements (structured evidence review against pre-specified indicators, submitted by a party without a conflict of interest) satisfy the independence and methodology requirements that 45 CFR 2522.710 imposes on the independent evaluation.

**Multi-year phased reporting and CROSS completion gates:** The year-one output/year-two-to-three outcome reporting sequence corresponds to CROSS's completion gate structure applied across a multi-year grant cycle. CROSS programs operating under AmeriCorps funding declare which gate applies at each annual reporting point: the year-one gate is a Build completion gate (outputs documented), and the year-two and year-three gates are Change completion gates (outcomes documented against the baseline established in year one).

| Part 2522 Requirement | CROSS Provision |
|:--|:--|
| Mandatory pre-award logic model | Entry specification gate (Theory of Change hierarchy declared before application opens) |
| Logic model inputs, activities, outputs, outcomes | Theory of Change hierarchy (all four levels required at entry gate) |
| Nationally prescribed output/outcome pairing menu | Build + Change obligation pairing (output commitment requires matched outcome commitment) |
| Year-one output reporting | Build completion gate (output evidence reviewed at defined interval) |
| Year-two and year-three outcome reporting | Change completion gate (outcome evidence against year-one baseline) |
| Internal performance reporting below $500K | Attestation gate (program-produced structured evidence review) |
| Independent evaluation at and above $500K | Independent attestation pathway (third-party evidence submission required) |
| Independent evaluator conflict-of-interest requirement | Attestation gate independence requirement |
| Competitive reapplication evaluation submission | Attestation corpus (independent evidence accessible to reviewing authority) |

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

The AmeriCorps national output/outcome pairing menu specifies what must be measured at the nationally defined category level but does not specify how individual programs must operationally define the measurement instruments at the local level. A program selecting the "education outcomes" pairing, for example, receives the national definition of the output and outcome categories but must translate those categories into local intake fields, data collection instruments, and evidence thresholds without further specification from the pairing menu. This translation step is where WALKRI's pre-publication requirements apply.

WALKRI's operational definition requirement, applied to each local intake field before any member begins service, forces the program to specify what the nationally defined output or outcome means in terms of the program's specific local intervention. The evidence form requirement specifies what data source documents the output (attendance records, tutoring session logs, assessment scores) and what the collection method is. The compliance threshold requirement specifies what level of output or outcome at what point in the program year constitutes satisfactory progress against the declared logic model commitment. Without this specification, programs using the same national pairing menu produce results that appear comparable at the category level but reflect different local definitions, different assessment instruments, and different thresholds.

For programs above the $500,000 independent evaluation threshold, the WALKRI pre-publication audit produces the instrument-level documentation that independent evaluators need to assess whether the program's local measurement practices correspond to the national pairing definitions. An independent evaluator reviewing a WALKRI-conformant program finds each local intake field pre-specified with its operational definition, evidence form, and compliance threshold documented before any data was collected. This reduces the evaluation burden and removes the most common source of evaluator-program disagreement: retrospective disputes about what the program intended to measure.
