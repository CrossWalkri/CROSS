# Wellcome Trust Grant Conditions and Open Access Policy

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

Wellcome (London, UK) is one of the world's largest independent research funders, distributing GBP 1 billion or more annually in biomedical and global health research funding. Wellcome's published Grant Conditions impose formally documented requirements applicable to UK and overseas awards alike: mandatory annual and post-grant outcomes reporting across 16 output types via Researchfish (a system Wellcome announced it will discontinue after the 2025/26 submission period, with a replacement pending), mandatory open access publication under Wellcome's Open Access Policy (effective January 2021), mandatory data availability statements and data sharing for all research outputs, IP and Commercialization Reports required until expiry of any IP created with Wellcome funding, and withholding of the final 10% of payment until final expenditure and end-of-grant reports are submitted. CROSS+WALKRI satisfies the Wellcome Grant Conditions structurally, with two specific accommodations: the open access and data sharing requirements are outside CROSS's core scope and must be added as explicit gate criteria, and the IP reporting obligation extends CROSS's attestation corpus concept beyond the standard grant period.

---

## The Wellcome Grant Conditions Framework

Wellcome's Grant Conditions constitute one of the more demanding documentation regimes among large independent research funders. The 16 Researchfish output types span a breadth that reflects Wellcome's commitment to tracking research impact beyond peer-reviewed publication: the categories include research papers and reports, intellectual property, collaborations and partnerships, further funding attracted, datasets and databases, software and technical products, research tools and methods, facilities and infrastructure, influence on policy, public engagement activities, awards and recognition, use in teaching, spin-outs, and related categories. This breadth reflects Wellcome's theory that biomedical research produces value across a wide range of channels, and that a funder tracking only publications is missing most of what its funding produces.

The Open Access Policy (effective January 2021) requires that all research articles and reviews supported by Wellcome be published open access with a CC BY license, either through immediate open access publication in a compliant journal or through deposit in an approved repository within six months of publication. The data availability statement requirement mandates that all publications include a statement specifying where the underlying data can be accessed and under what conditions. The data sharing policy extends this requirement to the data itself: underlying research data must be made available no later than the point of publication of the associated article.

The IP reporting obligation creates a post-grant accountability tail that distinguishes Wellcome from most other funders in this series. IP and Commercialization Reports must be submitted annually until any IP created with Wellcome funding reaches its natural expiry, which in the case of pharmaceutical patents can be 20 years or more. This obligation persists regardless of whether the original grant has been closed, the institution has changed affiliation, or the original research team has dispersed. The final payment withholding (the final 10% is withheld until final expenditure and end-of-grant reports are submitted) creates a hard disbursement gate at the close of the grant period.

## How CROSS Satisfies the Wellcome Grant Conditions

CROSS satisfies the core Wellcome Grant Conditions requirements at the structural level, with explicit accommodations for the requirements that extend beyond CROSS's standard scope.

**Completion gate and annual outcomes reporting mandate:** Wellcome's mandatory annual outcomes reporting across 16 Researchfish output types corresponds to CROSS's completion gate evidence requirement: outputs and outcomes are documented against pre-specified categories before any grant period is closed. For annual reporting, CROSS's interim completion gate structure applies: at each reporting interval, the grantee submits evidence against the pre-specified indicator set (the 16 Researchfish categories) before the next period of funding is confirmed. The 16 output categories are the pre-specified indicator set for a Wellcome-funded program; they are established by Wellcome before any application is submitted and cannot be redesigned by the grantee.

**Disbursement gate and final payment withholding:** Wellcome's withholding of the final 10% of payment until final expenditure and end-of-grant reports are submitted corresponds directly to CROSS's completion gate as a disbursement gate: the final payment tranche is contingent on evidence submission. This is one of the few funder conditions in this compatibility series that creates a hard financial consequence for report non-submission, rather than relying on future funding eligibility as the enforcement mechanism. CROSS's completion gate formalizes this structure: the gate must be cleared before the final disbursement occurs.

**Attestation corpus and post-grant IP reporting:** The IP and Commercialization Report obligation until IP expiry extends CROSS's attestation corpus concept beyond the grant period. The attestation corpus for a Wellcome-funded program includes not only the end-of-grant documentation but also the ongoing IP status records maintained for years or decades post-grant. CROSS's attestation corpus provisions, applied to Wellcome programs, must therefore include a long-tail attestation schedule that reflects the IP reporting obligation and is documented at the entry gate as a known post-grant accountability requirement.

**External standard reference mechanism for open access and data sharing:** Wellcome's open access publication requirement and data sharing policy are outside CROSS's core scope, which covers the grant program's obligation architecture rather than the intellectual property and publication regime of the research it funds. These requirements must be added as explicit gate criteria using CROSS's external standard reference mechanism (Part IV of the CROSS specification): the completion gate documentation for a Wellcome-funded program must include a checklist item confirming open access compliance and data availability statement submission, referenced to the Wellcome Open Access Policy as an external standard. This mechanism preserves CROSS's modularity while ensuring that the full Wellcome compliance requirement is reflected in the gate structure.

| Mandatory annual outcomes reporting across 16 Researchfish output types | Completion gate evidence against pre-specified indicator set (16 output categories) |
| 16 output categories established by Wellcome before application | Entry specification gate (indicator set pre-specified by funder) |

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI's five pre-publication requirements address the Researchfish outcome category specification problem that is the primary data quality weakness in Wellcome's outcomes reporting system. The 16 Researchfish output types are defined at the category level by Wellcome, but the operational definitions of what counts as a "collaboration," a "dataset," a "policy influence event," or a "public engagement activity" are not specified at the field level within the Researchfish instrument. This produces a classic intake specification failure: grantees record outputs in the categories that seem most plausible given their understanding of the definitions, producing data that appears to use a common taxonomy but is not in practice commensurable across programs.

WALKRI's five requirements applied to each of the 16 Researchfish output categories before any grantee completes an intake field would resolve this at source. The criterion intent field establishes what Wellcome is trying to track with each category and why. The operational definition field specifies exactly what qualifies: what makes an interaction a "collaboration" (joint publication? formal partnership agreement? shared data? informal consultation?) and what does not. The response form field specifies what the grantee actually records and in what format. The evidence form field specifies what documentation is required to support the recorded output (a signed agreement, a publication with both institutions listed, an email chain). The compliance threshold field specifies what level of engagement or output production constitutes a reportable event, distinguishing substantive outcomes from incidental contacts. Applied before any grantee submits their first annual report, these requirements produce a Researchfish dataset that is commensurable across Wellcome's portfolio, supporting the cross-program analysis that the 16-category system is designed to enable.
