# EU Structural and Investment Funds (ESIF) and Cohesion Policy (CPR)

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

The EU Structural and Investment Funds (ESIF), governed by the Common Provisions Regulation (CPR) 2021/1060, constitute one of the world's largest grant programs by volume, covering regional development, employment, and social inclusion across EU member states. The CPR operates under a formally distinct European regulatory evaluation architecture that is not covered by the EU Global Europe Results Framework (EU GERF), which governs only external development aid. CROSS+WALKRI satisfies the CPR's evaluation requirements structurally: the five CPR evaluation criteria map to CROSS's core architecture, and the mandatory common indicator sets required by each fund's regulation are satisfied by CROSS's eleven-field indicator specification.

---

## The EU Cohesion Policy Evaluation Framework

The EU Cohesion Policy 2021-2027, governed by the Common Provisions Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2021/1060), is the primary framework for EU structural funding covering the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF), the Cohesion Fund, the European Social Fund+ (ESF+), the Just Transition Fund (JTF), and related instruments. The CPR establishes formal evaluation requirements that differ structurally from the EU Global Europe Results Framework (EU GERF), which covers only EU external development cooperation (DG INTPA). ESIF and EU GERF are parallel EU regulatory frameworks for different contexts: ESIF governs internal EU cohesion spending; GERF governs external aid.

The CPR evaluation framework has four distinctive elements not found in OECD DAC or other covered frameworks:

**Five CPR evaluation criteria:** The CPR applies five evaluation criteria: relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, coherence, and EU added value. The "EU added value" criterion is structurally distinct from OECD DAC, which applies impact and sustainability as separate criteria. EU added value requires that programs demonstrate what the EU-level investment produces beyond what member states would have achieved without EU funding: it is a mandatory additionality criterion codified in the regulation.

**Mandatory common output and result indicators:** Each fund regulation (ERDF, ESF+, JTF, Cohesion Fund) codifies mandatory common output and result indicators in its regulatory annexes. These indicators must be reported by all Managing Authorities across all 27 member states, producing a cross-cohesion comparable dataset that is among the most consistent monitoring datasets in any public grants system worldwide. The ESF+ Common Indicators Toolbox specifies participant-level indicators (covering age, gender, employment status, education level, household situation) that are mandatory across all national ESF+ programmes.

**Programme-level evaluation plans:** CPR Article 45 requires Managing Authorities to produce evaluation plans at programme start, specifying what evaluations will be conducted and when. This ex-ante evaluation planning requirement is mandatory and published.

**Performance framework:** The CPR's performance framework (SWD(2021) 198) requires milestones and targets for each programme's output and result indicators, assessed at mid-term (2025) with potential reprogramming consequences. This creates a mandatory mid-term accountability gate with financial consequences for underperformance.

## How CROSS Satisfies the CPR Evaluation Framework

CROSS addresses all five CPR evaluation criteria and the mandatory indicator infrastructure at the structural level.

**EU added value criterion:** The EU added value criterion requires programs to demonstrate the counterfactual: what would have happened without EU funding? This is the regulatory form of CROSS's causality stance field and additionality argument. CROSS requires programs to declare at the entry specification gate whether they claim attribution or contribution, and to specify the counterfactual baseline against which change is measured. A CROSS-conformant program funded under ESIF can use the causality stance field to document the additionality argument that the "EU added value" criterion requires: what the EU contribution produces that member state action alone would not have produced.

**Relevance criterion:** Corresponds to CROSS's population scope declaration and Theory of Change goal declaration: who the program serves and what long-term condition it is designed to contribute to, both of which must be declared before applications open.

**Effectiveness criterion:** Corresponds to CROSS's completion gate: the structured evidence review against pre-specified indicators that determines whether the program produced what it stated it would produce.

**Efficiency criterion:** Corresponds to CROSS's concurrent funding disclosure and the resource documentation in CROSS's organizational identity fields: what resources were applied to produce the measured results.

**Coherence criterion:** Corresponds to CROSS's coherence disclosure: how the program relates to other interventions in the same problem area and to other EU policy instruments in the same funding context.

**Mandatory common indicators:** The CPR's mandatory common output and result indicators are pre-specified by the fund regulations. CROSS's eleven-field indicator specification (Fields 1-5: name, definition, unit, baseline, target) directly satisfies the mandatory indicator documentation requirements: a CROSS-conformant program using mandatory common indicators has already specified them with all five required elements as a structural consequence of conformance. For ESF+ participant-level indicators, CROSS's population scope declaration and disaggregation ratchet requirement produce the demographic disaggregation (age, gender, employment status) that the ESF+ Common Indicators Toolbox requires.

**Programme-level evaluation plans:** The CPR's mandatory evaluation plan requirement corresponds to CROSS's entry specification gate: the evaluation plan's structure (what evaluations will be conducted and when) is specified before any application opens, with the gate criteria constituting the measurable standards against which each evaluation will assess results.

| CPR Evaluation Element | CROSS Provision |
|:--|:--|
| EU added value criterion | Causality stance field + additionality argument + counterfactual baseline requirement |
| Relevance criterion | Population scope declaration + Theory of Change goal declaration |
| Effectiveness criterion | Completion gate evidence against pre-specified indicators |
| Efficiency criterion | Concurrent funding disclosure + organizational identity resource documentation |
| Coherence criterion | Coherence disclosure requirement |
| Mandatory common indicator sets | Eleven-field indicator specification (Fields 1-5 satisfy all mandatory indicator documentation elements) |
| ESF+ participant-level disaggregation | Disaggregation ratchet requirement (demographic categories established and maintained across reporting periods) |
| CPR Article 45 evaluation plans | Entry specification gate (evaluation structure published before applications open) |
| Performance framework milestones and targets | CROSS Fields 7-9 (baseline, target, milestone declaration at entry gate) |

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

The CPR's mandatory common indicator sets create a specific challenge for WALKRI compliance: the indicators are pre-specified by regulation and cannot be redesigned by individual Managing Authorities. For mandatory indicators, the WALKRI pre-publication audit confirms that the operational definitions, response forms, and evidence requirements from the regulatory annexes are faithfully reproduced in the programme's intake instruments before any beneficiary sees the form. For programme-specific additional indicators (which Managing Authorities may add beyond the mandatory common set), WALKRI's full five-requirement audit applies.

The ESF+ participant-level common indicators require particularly precise field specification. An ESF+ intake field for "employment status at entry" must specify exactly which employment states qualify, how mixed-status households are classified, and what documentary evidence is required. Without WALKRI-level specification, reviewers across managing authorities interpret the same regulatory indicator differently, producing data that appears comparable (it uses the same mandatory indicator) but is not in practice. WALKRI's operational definition requirement, applied before any participant registers, resolves this at source.
