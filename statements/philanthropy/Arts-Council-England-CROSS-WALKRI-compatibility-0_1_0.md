# Arts Council England Let's Create Investment Principles Compatibility

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

Arts Council England's four Investment Principles and their associated annual reporting obligations map directly onto CROSS's goal-level declaration and progress verification gate architecture. The Investment Principles Plan, submitted annually by National Portfolio Organisations and Investment Principle Support Organisations, is structurally equivalent to a CROSS multi-dimensional outcome declaration: each Principle functions as a goal-level commitment with its own evidence trajectory, and WALKRI's intake field specification ensures that evidence collection for each Principle is a well-specified measurement instrument rather than an open narrative prompt.

Arts Council England's "Let's Create" strategy (2020-2030) governs the funding of over 900 organisations through the National Portfolio Organisation (NPO) and Investment Principle Support Organisation (IPSO) programmes. The four Investment Principles are: Ambition and Quality, Dynamism, Environmental Responsibility, and Inclusivity and Relevance. Compliance with these Principles is a formal funding obligation enforced by Arts Council England through annual Investment Principles Plan submissions. CROSS+WALKRI provides a conformant documentation architecture that satisfies these obligations as structural consequences of gate configuration.

---

## Arts Council England's Approach

The Let's Create strategy represents a deliberate shift by Arts Council England from input and output measurement toward outcome and impact evidence. Rather than asking funded organisations to report on how many activities they delivered, the Investment Principles framework asks them to demonstrate how their work is achieving change across four substantive dimensions: the quality and ambition of the work itself, the organisation's capacity for change and learning, its environmental conduct, and its relationship with communities that have historically been underserved by arts and culture funding.

Each of the four Principles carries a distinct evidentiary logic. Ambition and Quality asks for evidence about the character of the work, including peer recognition, critical response, audience engagement, and the organisation's own reflection on quality standards. Dynamism addresses organisational learning, adaptation, and collaborative capacity. Environmental Responsibility requires evidence of progress against environmental targets and an active environmental policy. Inclusivity and Relevance requires evidence of meaningful engagement with diverse communities, workforce diversity, and programming that reflects the communities in the organisation's geography.

The annual Investment Principles Plan is the primary accountability instrument. Organisations are expected to map their planned and actual activity against all four Principles, providing both qualitative and quantitative evidence of progress. Arts Council England programme officers review these submissions and may follow up with questions or flag concerns about alignment. Persistent misalignment between stated commitments and evidence of progress is a basis for Arts Council England intervention, including funding modification.

The requirement to submit evidence against all four Principles simultaneously, rather than selecting a subset most favorable to the organisation, is architecturally significant. It means that every funded organisation must maintain active measurement and learning systems across all four dimensions throughout the funding period, not only in the run-up to a reporting deadline.

## How CROSS Satisfies the Let's Create Investment Principles

| Investment Principle | CROSS Provision | Assessment |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| Ambition and Quality | Goal-level declaration in CROSS Theory of Change hierarchy; qualitative evidence form in indicator specification | Full satisfaction. Each Investment Principle functions as a goal-level commitment in the CROSS ToC. Ambition and Quality indicators can specify qualitative evidence forms (peer review, critical documentation, audience reflection) without being forced into numeric formats. |
| Dynamism | Progress verification gate architecture; developmental gate character for adaptive learning indicators | Full satisfaction. CROSS's progress verification gate is designed for intermediate check-ins during implementation, precisely the point at which evidence of organisational learning and adaptation accumulates. Developmental gate character supports inference about direction of change rather than requiring target attainment at each check-in. |
| Environmental Responsibility | Separate outcome indicator with own compliance threshold; evidence form specified as environmental policy document and progress record | Full satisfaction. CROSS's multi-dimensional outcome declaration capability allows Environmental Responsibility to carry its own indicator specification, with a compliance threshold set at Arts Council England's minimum expectation. WALKRI's evidence form field specifies what artifact (environmental policy, carbon footprint record) constitutes acceptable evidence. |
| Inclusivity and Relevance | Population field in indicator specification; evidence form accommodating community testimony and workforce data | Full satisfaction. CROSS Field 6 (population) captures the specificity of community engagement claims. WALKRI's response form field can specify both quantitative (workforce diversity percentage) and qualitative (community co-design record) evidence types without privileging one over the other. |
| Annual evidence submission (all four Principles) | Progress verification gate record; CROSS Attestation Corpus | Full satisfaction. CROSS's progress verification gate outputs constitute a time-stamped record of what evidence was submitted, against which criteria, and what determination was reached. The Attestation Corpus aggregates these records across the funding period, producing the documentation trail that Arts Council England's annual review process requires. |

**Result:** CROSS satisfies all four Investment Principles and the annual reporting obligation as structural consequences of gate configuration. Organisations using CROSS+WALKRI do not need to maintain a parallel reporting system for Arts Council England compliance; the gate record IS the Investment Principles Plan evidence base.

The multi-dimensional nature of the Investment Principles framework is particularly well served by CROSS's architecture. Because the four Principles are genuinely distinct evidentiary domains rather than sub-indicators of a single outcome, they require separate indicator specifications, separate evidence forms, and separate compliance thresholds. CROSS's ability to support multiple outcome-level indicators simultaneously, each with its own field-level specification, matches the structural requirement of the Investment Principles Plan without forcing false aggregation across Principles.

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI's role in the Arts Council England context is to ensure that the intake fields collecting evidence for each Investment Principle are measurement instruments with well-specified criteria, not open-ended narrative prompts. The Investment Principles Plan, as a reporting format, creates a risk: organisations asked to narrate their progress against broad Principles may produce responses that are genuine but not comparable, and programme officers reviewing those responses may reach different judgments depending on what they look for.

WALKRI's pre-publication audit requirement addresses this risk at source. When Arts Council England or a CROSS-conformant programme officer designs the intake fields that collect Investment Principles Plan evidence, WALKRI requires that each field specify what it is measuring (criterion intent), how the criterion is bounded (operational definition), what format the response should take (response form), what artifact constitutes evidence (evidence form), and what the minimum standard is (compliance threshold). These five specifications transform a narrative prompt into a measurement instrument. Organisations know exactly what is being assessed. Reviewers apply consistent standards. The resulting evidence base is more comparable across organisations and across funding periods.

For NPOs and IPSOs using CROSS+WALKRI, the Investment Principles Plan ceases to be a separate reporting exercise and becomes a scheduled gate event within an ongoing CROSS-conformant documentation process. Annual evidence submission is the progress verification gate. The four Principles are the four outcome dimensions. WALKRI-conformant intake fields are the evidence collection instruments. The gate record is the Investment Principles Plan submission.
