# EVPA / Impact Europe Five-Step Impact Measurement Guide Compatibility

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

The European Venture Philanthropy Association (EVPA, now Impact Europe) published "A Practical Guide to Measuring and Managing Impact" in 2013, updated 2015, establishing a five-step impact measurement model used across European venture philanthropy and social investment. CROSS satisfies all five steps structurally, with each step corresponding to one or more CROSS gate conditions or field requirements. WALKRI applies at Steps 1 and 2, ensuring that the objectives and stakeholder fields those steps require are measurement instruments rather than labels.

The EVPA guide is the primary published impact measurement standard for European venture philanthropy. Its 2019 companion document, "EVPA Impact Management Principles," co-published with Social Value International, bridges to SVI's Principles of Social Value and to the Social Return on Investment methodology. Because CROSS+WALKRI maintains a separate compatibility statement for SROI, the EVPA-SVI bridge means that programs satisfying CROSS+WALKRI satisfy the EVPA guide and its SVI alignment through a single structural conformance.

---

## The EVPA Five-Step Model

The EVPA guide defines impact measurement as a five-step cycle applied to both grantees and investees, treating measurement requirements as equivalent regardless of the financial instrument:

Step 1: Set objectives. The organization and its investee define what change they are trying to achieve, for whom, within what timeframe, and at what scale.

Step 2: Analyze stakeholders. The measurement process identifies whose lives are affected, which stakeholder groups are primary and secondary, and how each group's perspective will be incorporated into the measurement design.

Step 3: Measure results. The organization collects evidence of actual changes against the objectives declared at Step 1, using indicators specified before data collection begins.

Step 4: Verify and value impact. Collected evidence is assessed for attribution or contribution, adjusted for counterfactuals, and translated into a judgment about whether the intended impact occurred.

Step 5: Monitor and report. Results and the verification process are documented, shared with relevant stakeholders, and used to inform future funding decisions.

The guide emphasizes that Steps 1 and 2 are specification-layer steps: the quality of Steps 3 through 5 depends directly on the quality of the objectives and stakeholder analysis established in Steps 1 and 2. Poor specification at the entry point produces data that cannot support the verification and valuation required at Step 4.

## How CROSS Satisfies the Five-Step Model

CROSS's gate architecture corresponds directly to the five-step cycle. The correspondence is not analogical; it is structural. Each step in the EVPA model requires a specific artifact or decision, and CROSS produces that artifact or decision as a condition of gate passage.

| EVPA Step | CROSS Provision | Assessment |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 1. Set objectives | Entry specification gate: Fields 7-11 (goal declaration, outcome specification, output deliverable, activity scope, indicator specification); Theory of Change hierarchy declared before applications open | Full satisfaction. CROSS requires objective-setting as a gate precondition. The entry specification gate cannot pass without declared objectives at goal, outcome, and output levels with indicators attached. |
| 2. Analyze stakeholders | Population scope field (CROSS Field 6, applied at program and indicator level); coherence disclosure requirement (how the program relates to other interventions serving the same population) | Full satisfaction. CROSS requires population scope declaration as a specification-layer requirement, not a narrative element. Coherence disclosure addresses secondary stakeholder effects by requiring declaration of how the program interacts with other interventions serving the same groups. |
| 3. Measure results | Progress verification gate and completion gate; evidence submission against pre-specified indicators; gate character (developmental or summative) | Full satisfaction. CROSS's gate structure requires evidence submission against pre-specified indicators at defined milestones. Evidence must match the source and frequency declared in the entry specification. Deviation triggers a gate record, not an informal update. |
| 4. Verify and value impact | Causality stance field (Indicator Field 11): attribution or contribution declaration; independent attestation pathway; additionality argument requirement for attribution stance | Full satisfaction. CROSS Field 11 requires programs to declare whether they are claiming attribution or contribution for each indicator, with corresponding evidence requirements. The independent attestation pathway provides the verification layer. |
| 5. Monitor and report | Continuation gate: sustainability stance (sustained, conditional, dependent); Attestation Corpus; obligation record as versioned, auditable disclosure artifact | Full satisfaction. CROSS's continuation gate requires a sustainability judgment that is recorded, not merely asserted. The Attestation Corpus accumulates gate records across the program lifecycle, producing the longitudinal monitoring record the EVPA guide requires. |

**Result:** CROSS satisfies all five EVPA steps as structural consequences of conformance, not as additional documentation requirements imposed on top of a CROSS-conformant program.

## EVPA-SVI Bridge

The 2019 "EVPA Impact Management Principles," co-published with Social Value International, bridges the EVPA five-step model to SVI's Principles of Social Value. The SVI Principles include: involve stakeholders, understand what changes, value the things that matter, only include what is material, do not over-claim, be transparent, and verify the result.

CROSS's relationship to SROI and the SVI Principles is addressed in a separate compatibility statement. That statement establishes that CROSS's causality stance field, population scope declaration, coherence disclosure, and independent attestation pathway satisfy the SVI Principles structurally. Because the EVPA-SVI bridge makes EVPA conformance a consequence of SVI compliance, and because CROSS satisfies SVI principles through its gate architecture, the EVPA-SVI bridge adds no additional requirements for CROSS-conformant programs. Programs using CROSS that declare SROI alignment in Indicator Field 10 satisfy both the EVPA five-step model and the SVI Principles through the same structural provisions.

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI's five pre-publication field requirements apply most critically at Steps 1 and 2, where the EVPA guide emphasizes that specification quality determines everything downstream. WALKRI's criterion intent requirement ensures that each intake field collecting objectives data (Step 1) has a stated measurement purpose before any applicant encounters it. WALKRI's operational definition requirement ensures that the population scope fields collecting stakeholder data (Step 2) have explicit boundaries: who is included, who is excluded, and how inclusion will be verified.

WALKRI's evidence form requirement, applied to the indicators declared at Step 1, ensures that each indicator has a verification pathway independent of the grantee's self-report. This is the instrument-design expression of Step 4's verification requirement: verification cannot be a structural afterthought if the evidence form is specified before collection begins. Programs that have passed WALKRI's pre-publication audit before activating CROSS's entry specification gate have satisfied the EVPA guide's implicit but load-bearing requirement that Steps 1 and 2 produce measurement-grade specifications rather than narrative intentions.
