# Community Foundation National Standards (US)

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

The Community Foundation National Standards Board (cfstandards.org), supported by the Council on Foundations, administers the primary accreditation standard for US community foundations: 26 standards across five domains covering governance, resource development, stewardship, grantmaking, and donor relations. Over 900 accredited US community foundations collectively manage more than one hundred billion USD in assets, and reaccreditation is required every three years. CROSS+WALKRI satisfies the grantmaking, stewardship, and accountability requirements of the National Standards by providing the structured gate architecture and persistent attestation record the standards require.

---

## The Community Foundation National Standards' Approach

The National Standards exist to provide donor confidence and community trust in community foundations operating across the United States. Because community foundations hold and deploy donor funds toward charitable purposes, they face accountability requirements that private foundations and corporate grantmakers do not: they must demonstrate that funds are deployed only for qualified charitable purposes, that due diligence processes are documented and followed consistently, and that grantmaking decisions are responsive to the specific community the foundation serves.

The Grantmaking domain is the most operationally demanding from an evidence standpoint. Standards in this domain require that community foundations: respond to documented community needs (not only funder priorities); apply due diligence policies including expenditure responsibility for grants to non-public charities; and use grant funds only for qualified charitable purposes. These are not aspirational principles; they are conditions for accreditation reviewed during the three-year reaccreditation cycle.

The Stewardship and Accountability domain requires that foundation operations be documented in ways that support independent review. Grantmaking decisions must be traceable to stated policies. Conflicts of interest must be managed through documented procedures. Financial management must meet independent audit standards. These requirements collectively define a transparency and traceability standard that applies not only to financial records but to the grant decision-making process itself.

The Community Needs Responsiveness requirement addresses a specific accountability gap: community foundations may not simply deploy donor funds toward donor preferences if those preferences are not connected to documented community needs. This requirement distinguishes community foundations from donor-advised funds and private foundations: the "community" in community foundation implies an obligation to serve a defined geographic or demographic community's needs, and that connection must be demonstrable.

## How CROSS Satisfies the Community Foundation National Standards

CROSS provides the structured gate architecture and pre-commitment record that the Grantmaking and Stewardship domains require.

The due diligence policy requirement is addressed by CROSS's structured gate architecture. Each gate in a CROSS-conformant program has a documented evidentiary standard: what must be demonstrated, what forms of evidence are accepted, and what threshold of evidence triggers a favorable determination. Because these standards are published before submissions arrive, they constitute a documented due diligence policy in the sense the National Standards require. Reviewers follow a procedure that existed before the specific application was received, which is the structural form of due diligence as distinct from discretionary judgment applied case by case.

The expenditure responsibility requirement, which applies when community foundations grant to non-public charities and requires documenting that grant funds were used for the stated qualified charitable purpose, maps to CROSS's completion gate. Grantees demonstrate at the completion gate that stated deliverables were produced before continuation or final funding is released. This gate creates the documentation trail the expenditure responsibility standard requires: a record that the purpose stated in the grant agreement is the purpose for which funds were deployed, evidenced by the deliverables or condition changes the grantee committed to produce.

The community needs responsiveness requirement maps to CROSS's coherence disclosure combined with the population scope declaration. CROSS programs must specify the problem area being addressed and the population affected. When a community foundation uses CROSS, it must declare the community needs its grantmaking program addresses and the population for whom it is addressing them, before any application is evaluated. This creates a documented connection between grantmaking activity and community need that the National Standards require but do not specify how to produce.

The grantmaking transparency requirement is addressed by CROSS's pre-commitment record. Round specifications are published before application windows open. When a community foundation's reaccreditation review examines grantmaking policies, the pre-commitment record provides independently verifiable evidence that criteria existed before submissions arrived, that criteria were applied consistently, and that departures from stated criteria were documented. This is a stronger form of transparency than post-hoc policy documentation, which can be written to justify decisions already made.

The Stewardship and Accountability domain is addressed by CROSS's Attestation Corpus. The Attestation Corpus is a persistent, queryable record of all gate determinations and organizational identity claims made under a program. It provides the traceability the domain requires: any gate determination can be traced to the pre-committed specification that defined it, to the evidence reviewed, and to the reviewer's recorded rationale. This record persists across rounds and across program cycles, supporting the three-year reaccreditation review without requiring the foundation to reconstruct decision documentation after the fact.

| National Standards Requirement | CROSS Provision |
|---|---|
| Due diligence policy documentation | Gate architecture with pre-committed standards |
| Expenditure responsibility | Completion gate deliverable documentation |
| Community needs responsiveness | Coherence disclosure + population scope declaration |
| Grantmaking transparency | Pre-commitment record |
| Stewardship and accountability | Attestation Corpus (persistent, queryable) |

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI ensures that the intake forms used in community foundation grantmaking programs produce measurement-grade data, not merely labeled data. When the National Standards require evidence that a program responds to community needs, the evidence must be grounded in intake fields that actually collect what they claim to collect: information about the need, the population, and the proposed response. WALKRI's pre-publication audit verifies that each field has an operational definition, specifying what evidence a grantee must provide and how that evidence will be used.

For reaccreditation purposes, this matters because the National Standards review process examines not only outcomes but policies and procedures. A foundation whose intake forms were designed using WALKRI's audit procedure can demonstrate, in its reaccreditation materials, that its grantmaking intake was designed as a measurement instrument rather than an administrative convenience. This is a form of procedural evidence that supports accreditation independent of any particular grant outcome.
