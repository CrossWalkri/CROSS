# Wikimedia Foundation Grants Committee Review Process and Framework

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

The Wikimedia Foundation administers six grant programs (Rapid Fund, General Support Fund, Conference and Event Fund, Wikimedia Hub Fund, Wikimedia Alliances Fund, and Wikimedia Research Fund) under a single published Committee Review Process and Framework on Meta-Wiki. Funding decisions are made by eight Regional Funds Committees, composed of experienced and newer community members, each with jurisdiction over a defined geographic region of the global Wikimedia movement. The review framework specifies four evaluation dimensions (Knowledge Equity Impact, Volunteer Community Value, Movement Value, and Feasibility) and a structured multi-stage review process anchored in the Wikimedia 2030 Movement Strategy. CROSS+WALKRI is compatible with this framework: the gate architecture accommodates committee-based decision making without prescribing a centralized reviewer model, and the indicator specification machinery accommodates the four-dimension review structure as a multi-criteria gate configuration.

---

## The Wikimedia Foundation Grants Framework

The Wikimedia Foundation's grant programs are distinguished from foundation-led and government-led grantmaking frameworks already covered in Part XII by being movement-governed rather than institutionally-governed. Funding decisions are not made by Foundation staff acting as program officers in a traditional grantmaking hierarchy. Decisions are made by Regional Funds Committees populated by volunteers drawn from the Wikimedia community, with Community Resources staff serving in a coordination and due-diligence role rather than a decision role. This is a structurally distinct decision-standing lineage: the obligation directions run from grantees to the volunteer movement that the Foundation serves, with committee deliberation against published dimensions as the verification procedure and re-application, conditional funding, or no funding as the named response to non-conformance. The direction is not from grantees to Foundation staff acting on behalf of trustees.

The Committee Review Process and Framework, published on Meta-Wiki, specifies the review structure for the General Support Fund and applies in adapted form to other Wikimedia Community Fund programs. The framework defines four evaluation dimensions. The Knowledge Equity Impact dimension assesses whether the proposed work involves underrepresented groups (women, people with disabilities, indigenous communities, LGBTQ+ individuals, minority language speakers, underrepresented regions) and contributes to closing knowledge gaps. The Volunteer Community Value dimension assesses community involvement in planning and implementation, safety and inclusivity measures, volunteer feedback collection, and recruitment and retention activities. The Movement Value dimension assesses strategic alignment with the Wikimedia 2030 Movement Strategy Recommendations, partnerships enabling scalability, and transferable methodologies. The Feasibility dimension assesses realistic scope, achievable timelines, team capacity, and budget justification.

The review process operates through ten distinct stages: applicant submission via the grantee portal, due diligence by Community Resources program officers, individual committee member review against the four dimensions, posting of initial clarifying questions on Meta-Wiki, production of a unified structured feedback document, staff analysis shared with committee members, optional committee-applicant meetings, applicant revisions within published timeframes, formal deliberations, and decision notification through email, the Fluxx grants management system, and Meta-Wiki. The five funding recommendation outcomes are full funding, partial funding, no funding, deferred decision pending applicant discussion, and deferred decision pending committee meeting.

Eight Regional Funds Committees correspond to the regions Central and Eastern Europe and Central Asia, Northern and Western Europe, North America, Latin America and Caribbean, East Southeast Asia and Pacific, South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa, and Middle East and North Africa. Committee sizes typically range from five to ten members. New members are co-selected by existing committee members and the Community Resources Team. Eligibility requires active Wikimedia community membership or affiliate board experience, regional expertise, and alignment with movement strategy. Wikimedia Foundation staff, affiliate staff, and parties receiving compensation from grants are categorically barred from committee service. Members serve two-to-four-year terms with rolling membership and receive a small stipend.

The framework is bound to the Wikimedia 2030 Movement Strategy, which articulates two strategic directions: Knowledge Equity (focusing efforts on knowledge and communities left out by structures of power and privilege) and Knowledge as a Service (becoming the essential infrastructure of the ecosystem of free knowledge). The Knowledge Equity Impact dimension of the review framework operationalizes the first strategic direction at the proposal level. Grant alignment with Movement Strategy Recommendations is one of the four review dimensions, not a peripheral consideration.

Reporting obligations differ by program. General Support Fund grantees submit a written final learning report within thirty days of grant completion and participate in a mid-grant conversation report with their Regional Program Officer. Multi-year grants require annual reports using the same forms. Grantees with budgets exceeding regional thresholds must provide detailed financial reports or external audits. The Universal Code of Conduct applies to all individuals in grantee leadership roles as a condition of eligibility.

## How CROSS Satisfies the Wikimedia Committee Review Process and Framework

CROSS provides the gate architecture and obligation mode classification that the four-dimension review framework requires, while remaining agnostic to the committee-based decision structure.

The four review dimensions map to CROSS's gate criterion specification structure as a multi-criterion entry specification gate configuration. The Knowledge Equity Impact dimension maps to CROSS's public benefit mechanism declaration combined with the affected population specification: applicants declaring a Knowledge Equity Impact contribution must specify which underrepresented population the work serves, what mechanism delivers benefit to that population, and what access condition holds. The Volunteer Community Value dimension maps to CROSS's affected-population engagement and beneficiary validation dimension, which requires that the need for the deliverable or the claimed condition be validated by parties outside the applicant's organizational control. The Movement Value dimension maps to CROSS's coherence disclosure and pathway-level convergence analysis: alignment with Movement Strategy Recommendations is a convergence claim, and CROSS's Portfolio Position primitive distinguishes initiating, continuation, and convergence rounds. The Feasibility dimension maps to CROSS's institutional capacity dimension and the sufficiency architecture declaration, which together address scope realism, team capacity, and resource adequacy.

The committee-based decision structure is accommodated by CROSS's format-agnostic specification principle. CROSS specifies what must be done at each gate, not who must do it. A funder operating a CROSS-conformant program may configure the gate decision authority as an individual program officer, a staff panel, a volunteer committee, a hybrid of these, or any other named mechanism. The gate determination must be documented, the evidentiary basis must be traceable to pre-committed criteria, and the decision must be subject to the funder obligations in Part XI. Regional Funds Committees satisfy this requirement directly: their decisions are recorded against the four-dimension framework, the framework is published before any applicant submits, and the decision options are enumerated in advance.

The Wikimedia 2030 Movement Strategy alignment requirement maps to CROSS's coherence disclosure combined with the program-level Theory of Change pathway registry. A funder whose round configuration references the Movement Strategy Recommendations as an external standard activates the conformance threshold specification requirement in WALKRI: the round configuration must specify which Recommendations apply, what evidence satisfies each, and what the minimum threshold for passage is. Without this specification, "alignment with movement strategy" functions as a rhetorical commitment rather than an assessable criterion, which is the failure mode WALKRI is designed to prevent.

The multi-program structure of the Wikimedia Community Fund (Rapid Fund through Hub Fund) maps to CROSS's obligation mode classification. The Rapid Fund, which supports short-term low-cost projects at $500 to $5,000 USD over one to twelve months, is structurally a build-obligation program: the obligation object is the specified deliverable. The General Support Fund, which supports sustained organizational work starting at $10,000 USD, may operate in build, change, or hybrid obligation modes depending on the proposal, which is exactly the configurability CROSS's Part III specifies. The Wikimedia Research Fund, offering $2,000 to $50,000 USD for research proposals, may operate in build or change obligation modes depending on whether the research produces a specified output (a dataset, a paper, a model) or a measurable change in a research-relevant condition.

The reporting obligations (final learning report and mid-grant conversation report) map to CROSS's progress verification gate and completion verification gate. The mid-grant conversation report is structurally a progress verification gate with self-report-plus-documentation evidence strength. The final learning report is structurally a completion verification gate. The Universal Code of Conduct conformance requirement for leadership maps to the disbursement authority's named-person obligation: the named persons bound to grant obligations are also bound to a published behavioral standard, which the entry gate confirms.

The categorical bar on committee service by Foundation staff, affiliate staff, and parties receiving grant compensation maps to CROSS's Tier 1 conflict of interest classification, which is a categorical bar with no waiver. The Wikimedia framework operationalizes this at the committee selection stage rather than at the application review stage, which is a structurally stronger configuration: the conflict is removed from the decision pool before any specific application is reviewed.

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI ensures that the four review dimensions function as measurement instruments rather than rhetorical categories. A dimension such as Knowledge Equity Impact is not a measurement instrument unless each criterion under it specifies what construct it measures, what response form is appropriate, what evidence satisfies it, and what the conformance threshold is. WALKRI's five pre-publication field requirements apply to every criterion under each review dimension: criterion intent, operational definition, response form, evidence form, and conformance threshold must all be specified before the round opens and before any committee member begins review.

For the Wikimedia Movement Strategy alignment criterion specifically, WALKRI's conformance threshold requirement addresses a structural risk in movement-strategy-aligned grantmaking: a published strategy document with broad strategic directions can be cited as alignment without the citation carrying operational content. WALKRI requires that any field referencing the Movement Strategy Recommendations specify which Recommendations apply, what evidence satisfies alignment with each, and what the minimum threshold is. This converts strategic alignment from an interpretive judgment by individual committee members into a documented criterion against which the committee's collective determination can be recorded.

WALKRI also addresses the inter-rater reliability question that arises in committee review. When five to ten committee members each review a proposal against the four dimensions, the reliability of the assessment depends on whether all members are applying the same operational definitions. A field with a written operational definition produces more consistent assessments across reviewers than a field whose criteria are left to individual interpretation. WALKRI's operational definition requirement is therefore not only a precision requirement but also a reliability requirement, and committee-based decision structures benefit from it more than individual-reviewer structures do.

The framework's multi-stage review process (individual review, clarifying questions, structured feedback document, applicant meeting, formal deliberation) produces a documented decision trail. WALKRI's evidence form specification ensures that the artifacts produced at each stage (the structured feedback document, the staff analysis, the committee meeting record, the final determination) are specified before the round opens. A program that publishes the artifact types expected at each stage can demonstrate conformance to its stated procedure in a way that ad hoc documentation cannot.

## Notes on Lineage Distinctness

The Wikimedia Foundation grants framework is structurally distinct from the foundation-led frameworks (Annie E. Casey, Community Foundation National Standards, Wellcome Trust, Templeton) and government-led frameworks (USAID Evaluation Policy, GCF, IFC Performance Standards, SBIR/STTR) already covered in Part XII. The distinction is decision-standing lineage: Wikimedia grant decisions are made by volunteer committees drawn from the served population, not by paid program officers acting for trustees, and not by governmental evaluation officers acting for legislatures. This places Wikimedia grantmaking within the participatory-grantmaking tradition, alongside frameworks such as the Open Society Foundations' community advisory structures and the broader movement of trust-based and participatory philanthropy. CROSS's format-agnostic specification principle (Part I) was designed to accommodate this range of decision structures without forcing any of them into a centralized reviewer model.

The Wikimedia framework's binding of grant review to a movement-wide strategy (Wikimedia 2030) is also structurally distinct from the strategy-aligned frameworks of foundations operating their own strategies. Wikimedia 2030 was developed through a multi-year participatory strategy process across the global movement, not promulgated by the Foundation as institutional policy. Grant alignment with Movement Strategy Recommendations is therefore alignment with a movement-deliberated artifact, not with a foundation-internal strategic plan. CROSS's coherence disclosure mechanism accommodates either interpretation, and WALKRI's external standard conformance threshold requirement applies regardless of how the referenced strategy was produced.
