# UK Government Functional Standard GovS 015 (Grants) and Magenta Book Compatibility - CROSS+WALKRI

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

This statement documents structural alignment between the UK Cabinet Office Government Functional Standard GovS 015 (Grants), including its 2024 revisions, and the CROSS+WALKRI specification standards. GovS 015 governs how all UK central government departments administer grants; it is a funder-side standard, and its Minimum Requirement 8 (Performance and Monitoring) imposes structural obligations on departments before and after awards are made. CROSS v0.4.4 and WALKRI v0.1.7 satisfy the structural requirements of GovS 015 Minimum Requirement 8, the Government Evaluation Registry mandatory registration introduced in April 2024, the HM Treasury Magenta Book evaluation quality standards, and the Grants Continuous Improvement Assessment Framework.

---

## The GovS 015 and Magenta Book Framework

GovS 015 is published by the Cabinet Office and applies to all UK central government departments, their arm's-length bodies, and any organizations administering grants on behalf of a government department. The Standard sets twelve Minimum Requirements for compliant grant administration. Minimum Requirement 8, Performance and Monitoring, is the evaluation-relevant component: it mandates that performance measures and longer-term outcomes be defined for every grant and that active financial and performance management be maintained post-award.

The most distinctive feature of GovS 015 relative to all other national grant administration standards in CROSS+WALKRI's coverage is the Government Evaluation Registry. From 2 April 2024, all UK government evaluations, including evaluations of grant programs, must be registered on the Government Evaluation Registry before evaluation activity begins. The Registry is a mandatory public transparency mechanism: it creates a permanent, independently verifiable record that evaluation criteria and measurement commitments existed before grants were awarded. No other national grant framework imposes a pre-registration requirement of this kind.

The HM Treasury Magenta Book is the required evaluation methodology reference for all UK government evaluation. It sets quality standards for evaluation design, including standards for counterfactual specification, evidence strength, and proportionality of evaluation effort to program scale. Departments commissioning or overseeing evaluations of grants are expected to apply Magenta Book standards in determining evaluation design.

The Grants Continuous Improvement Assessment Framework provides a maturity model for grant-making organizations. It maps organizational capability across five levels and covers all phases of the grant lifecycle, from design through evaluation. The framework is directed at departments as institutions, not at individual grantees; in this respect it is a funder-side parallel to GFGP's organizational capacity tiers on the grantee side.

## How CROSS Satisfies GovS 015 and the Magenta Book

CROSS's entry specification gate is the primary structural response to Minimum Requirement 8. Minimum Requirement 8 requires that performance measures and longer-term outcomes be defined for every grant before awards are made. Under CROSS, no grant round may open without a published round specification that declares indicator specifications, Theory of Change architecture (goal, outcome, output, activity), evidence type requirements, and milestone structures. The entry specification gate is a precondition for the round opening, not a recommendation for good practice; this makes CROSS's compliance with Minimum Requirement 8 structural rather than aspirational.

The Government Evaluation Registry mandatory pre-registration requirement maps directly to CROSS's pre-commitment requirement and Attestation Corpus. CROSS requires that round specifications, including all evaluation criteria and indicator specifications, be published before applications open. Those specifications are preserved in the Attestation Corpus as persistent, independently verifiable records. Because the Attestation Corpus records are created before any application is submitted, they constitute exactly the class of pre-registration evidence the Government Evaluation Registry is designed to capture: independently verifiable evidence that evaluation criteria existed and were publicly documented before grants were awarded. A department implementing CROSS for grant administration satisfies the Registry's pre-registration requirement as a consequence of the CROSS gate process, without additional administrative burden.

The Magenta Book's evaluation quality standards map to CROSS's evidence strength taxonomy and WALKRI's five pre-publication field requirements at the intake layer. The Magenta Book emphasizes proportionality of evaluation design to program scale and clarity about counterfactual specification. CROSS's mandatory counterfactual baseline requirement (applicable in Change obligation mode) implements the Magenta Book's counterfactual specification standard. CROSS's evidence strength taxonomy, which distinguishes between pre-post measurement, matched comparison, and experimental comparison, implements the Magenta Book's evidence hierarchy in operational terms. The Magenta Book's proportionality principle is implemented by CROSS's obligation mode selection: Build obligation applies to programs where output verification is proportionate; Change obligation applies where outcome and causal evidence is required.

The Grants Continuous Improvement Assessment Framework's maturity model has no direct structural equivalent in CROSS, because the framework is directed at departments as institutions rather than at individual grant rounds. However, consistent implementation of CROSS across a department's grant portfolio is itself evidence of maturity at the higher levels of the framework: it demonstrates systematic pre-specification of performance measures, active post-award monitoring, and persistent evaluation records, all of which the framework recognizes as advanced capability.

The following table summarizes the core mappings.

| GovS 015 / Magenta Book Requirement | CROSS+WALKRI Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Minimum Requirement 8: performance measures and outcomes defined before award | CROSS entry specification gate: indicator specification and Theory of Change complete before applications open |
| Government Evaluation Registry mandatory pre-registration | CROSS pre-commitment requirement + Attestation Corpus: persistent public records of gate determinations |
| Magenta Book counterfactual specification standards | CROSS counterfactual baseline requirement (Change obligation mode) + evidence strength taxonomy |
| Magenta Book evaluation quality and proportionality | CROSS obligation mode selection + WALKRI five-field pre-publication audit |
| Continuous Improvement Assessment Framework maturity levels | Consistent CROSS implementation across portfolio as institutional evidence of advanced capability |

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI's five pre-publication field requirements ensure that the performance measures mandated by Minimum Requirement 8 are measurement instruments, not administrative labels. A field in a grant intake form that asks applicants to describe their intended outcomes is not a performance measure in the GovS 015 sense unless it specifies what constitutes an acceptable outcome, how the outcome will be measured, and what baseline the measurement is taken against. WALKRI's pre-publication audit requires that every intake field have its construct, metric, baseline, target, and evidence threshold specified before the field is presented to any applicant. This ensures that the performance measures departments define under Minimum Requirement 8 are operational before any application is submitted.

WALKRI also supports the Government Evaluation Registry pre-registration requirement directly. Because WALKRI requires measurement instrument specification before any applicant encounters a field, the instrument specifications are necessarily produced before the grant round opens. Those specifications, preserved through the CROSS Attestation Corpus, constitute the evaluation pre-registration record. A department implementing CROSS+WALKRI does not need a separate evaluation pre-registration workflow; the WALKRI audit produces the documentation the Registry requires as a byproduct of instrument design.
