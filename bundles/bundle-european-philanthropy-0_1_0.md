# CROSS+WALKRI Program Bundle: European Philanthropy

---

## Overview

This bundle covers grant and funding programs operating within European philanthropic, development finance, and EU regulatory contexts. The ecosystem spans impact investing and venture philanthropy, EU structural and cohesion fund grantmaking, European foundation and bilateral aid programs, and EU social enterprise impact measurement frameworks.

Four program sub-types operate in this space with materially different accountability structures, regulatory requirements, and measurement frameworks. This bundle addresses all four and notes where provisions diverge.

**Sub-type A: European impact investing and venture philanthropy** engages social enterprises and mission-driven organizations with a blend of financial return expectations and social outcome requirements. Key practitioners include EVPA (European Venture Philanthropy Association) members, Ashoka, and social enterprise support intermediaries. The EVPA five-step impact measurement model (plan, do, assess, review, share) is the embedded standard.

**Sub-type B: EU structural fund grantmaking** distributes resources from the European Structural and Investment Funds (ESIF) under the Common Provisions Regulation 2021/1060, covering the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF), European Social Fund Plus (ESF+), Just Transition Fund (JTF), and Cohesion Fund. Managing Authorities across 27 member states implement programmes using mandatory common indicator sets and performance frameworks with financial consequences at mid-term review.

**Sub-type C: European foundation grantmaking and Nordic bilateral aid** covers private European foundations using the PHINEO IOOI (Input-Output-Outcome-Impact) measurement framework, BMZ/GIZ German bilateral programming, AFD French Development Agency grants, and Nordic bilateral aid through Sida (Sweden), Norad (Norway), and Danida (Denmark), all operating under results-based management frameworks aligned with OECD DAC criteria with country-ownership emphasis.

**Sub-type D: EU social enterprise impact measurement** applies the EU GECES Social Impact Measurement framework (2014) and Social Bond reporting under ICMA Social Bond Principles to organizations generating commercial revenue alongside social outcomes.

## Part 1: CROSS Runbook

### Obligation Mode Selection

**Sub-type A (venture philanthropy):** Use Change obligation mode for programs designed to shift organizational capacity or social outcomes. Use Build obligation mode for grants funding a specific output (a technology platform, a publication, a specific service infrastructure). The EVPA five-step model's first step (plan) is the entry specification gate: Theory of Change, indicators, and impact goals must be defined before the grant is committed.

**Sub-type B (ESIF programs):** Use Change obligation mode. ESIF programmes are designed to produce output indicators (number of enterprises supported, number of persons trained) and result indicators (change in employment rate, change in GHG emissions) against pre-specified targets. The obligation is fulfilled when declared indicators are achieved against the targets set in the programme's performance framework. The EU added value criterion imposes an additionality requirement: the programme must demonstrate what EU-level investment produces beyond what member state action alone would have achieved.

**Sub-type C (European foundations and Nordic bilateral):** Use Change obligation mode aligned with OECD DAC criteria and results-based management frameworks. The PHINEO IOOI structure (for German-language foundations) maps the CROSS Theory of Change hierarchy directly: inputs and activities at the base, outputs one tier up, outcomes above that, and impact at the goal tier.

**Sub-type D (social enterprise):** Use Build or Change obligation mode depending on whether the grant funds a specific social enterprise infrastructure output or a measurable social outcome. The commercial revenue architecture must be disclosed at the entry gate; the social outcome obligation is distinct from the financial return expectation.

### Entry Specification Gate

Before any application window opens (or before a Managing Authority commits to a programme design for ESIF), the following must be committed to in writing and published in a verifiable location (programme document, foundation grant guidelines, published evaluation plan):

**Programme specification:**
- Obligation mode (Build, Change, or Retroactive)
- Total funding pool size and currency denomination
- Eligible programme or project categories (scope)
- Eligibility criteria (who may apply)
- Impact metrics: output indicators and result indicators with baseline values and targets
- Time period covered
- For ESIF programmes: CPR Article 45 evaluation plan, specifying what evaluations will be conducted and when

**EU added value declaration (Sub-type B):** The counterfactual must be specified at the entry gate: what would have happened in the absence of EU-level investment? Managing Authorities must state the baseline condition and articulate why EU investment produces outcomes beyond what member state budgets would have produced without the programme. This is not optional for ESIF programmes; it is a structural requirement of the CPR 2021/1060 evaluation framework.

**ESIF mandatory common indicator declaration (Sub-type B):** For each fund (ERDF, ESF+, JTF, Cohesion Fund), the regulatory annexes specify mandatory common output and result indicators that must be reported by all Managing Authorities. The programme design must declare which mandatory common indicators apply to each intervention field, and targets for each must be set before the programme opens. CROSS's eleven-field indicator specification satisfies the mandatory indicator documentation requirements as a structural consequence of conformance.

**ESF+ disaggregation declaration (Sub-type B):** ESF+ requires participant-level data collection disaggregated by age, gender, employment status (employed, self-employed, unemployed, long-term unemployed, inactive), educational attainment level, and household situation. The disaggregation categories and the data collection method must be declared at the entry gate.

**EVPA impact plan (Sub-type A):** Step 1 of the EVPA five-step model produces the impact plan: Theory of Change, impact goals, stakeholder map, indicators, and measurement plan. This document is the entry specification gate record for venture philanthropy grants; it must be completed and approved before any grant is committed.

**PHINEO IOOI plan (Sub-type C):** European foundations using the IOOI framework must complete the input-output-outcome-impact chain at the entry gate. The IOOI chain is the Theory of Change for the grant; outcome and impact indicators must be declared before funds are committed.

**Evaluation plan (Sub-type B, CPR Article 45):** Managing Authorities must produce an evaluation plan at programme start specifying: what evaluations will be conducted, when, by whom, and what questions each evaluation will address. This maps directly to CROSS's entry specification gate; the evaluation plan is the entry specification gate document for ESIF programmes.

### Application Gate

**Deliverable and output specification:** Each application must specify outputs with: a name, an operational definition, a unit of measurement, a target value, and the mandatory common indicator reference if one applies (Sub-type B). For ESIF-funded projects: the output indicators declared in the project application must correspond to the programme's mandatory common indicators.

**Result indicator baseline (Sub-type B):** Each result indicator requires a baseline value established before the project begins. Applications must include a baseline measurement plan if baseline data are not already available; baselines that are established after project activities begin do not satisfy the ESIF result indicator framework.

**Theory of Change with IOOI mapping (Sub-type C):** Applications to European foundations using IOOI must provide the full IOOI chain from the application's activities to the impact tier. Vague impact claims ("improving lives," "strengthening communities") that are not tied to specific, measurable outcomes in the IOOI chain do not satisfy the entry specification gate.

**Social enterprise revenue architecture (Sub-type A, D):** Applications must declare the commercial revenue model alongside the social mission. The relationship between revenue generation activities and social outcome activities must be specified: does the revenue model directly produce the social outcome, or does commercial revenue subsidize a separate social activity? This distinction affects the causality stance declaration.

**Additionality argument (Sub-type B):** Applications under ESIF programmes must address EU added value at the project level: what does this project produce beyond what the applicant or national public resources would have produced without EU funding? The additionality argument must be specific to the project's scope; a generic statement of EU programme alignment does not satisfy this field.

### Completion Gate

**Sub-type A (venture philanthropy):** Step 3 of the EVPA model (assess) is the completion gate. Evidence must confirm: declared output indicators achieved, outcome indicators assessed against targets, and a review (Step 4) that identifies learning to carry forward. The sharing step (Step 5) is the attestation gate: findings are published for the field.

**Sub-type B (ESIF):** Completion evidence addresses output and result indicators against the targets declared in the performance framework. For the mid-term performance review (2025 for 2021-2027 programmes): output indicator achievement against 2024 milestones determines whether funds are maintained or subject to reprogramming. This is portfolio-level accountability with financial consequences; milestone underachievement is not merely an evidence gap but may trigger fund reallocation.

**Sub-type C (European foundations and Nordic bilateral):** Completion evidence addresses outcomes in the IOOI chain: changes in participant conditions attributable to the programme's activities. Nordic results-based management frameworks require evidence of outcomes linked to the declared Theory of Change, with country-ownership evidence (partner government or civil society confirmation of results) for bilateral programmes.

**Sub-type D (social enterprise):** Completion evidence addresses both social outcome indicators and financial sustainability indicators. For ICMA Social Bond reporting: use-of-proceeds documentation, project selection and evaluation evidence, and impact reporting covering the funded period.

**Performance framework milestone review (Sub-type B):** ESIF programmes face a formal mid-term performance review in which the European Commission assesses whether programmes have achieved their 2024 milestones. This review functions as a portfolio-level completion gate assessment with financial consequences; programmes that significantly underachieve milestones risk performance reserve reallocation.

### Attestation Gate and Corpus

The Attestation Corpus for European philanthropy programs must address the regulatory specificity of ESIF programmes and the publication expectations of European foundations:

- Entry specification gate record: programme document or grant guidelines with all declared indicators, targets, and evaluation plan, version-controlled and publicly accessible
- ESIF evaluation plan (Sub-type B): CPR Article 45 evaluation plan published before programme activities begin
- Output and result indicator monitoring data: maintained throughout the grant period at the frequency declared in the performance framework
- ESF+ participant data (Sub-type B): disaggregated participant-level records maintained per ESIF data protection requirements and the relevant fund regulation's annex
- Completion gate assessment: programme evaluation report or grant final report addressing all declared indicators; for ESIF programmes, the annual performance report submitted to the European Commission
- EVPA five-step sharing output (Sub-type A): published case study or learning document per the share step

For Nordic bilateral programmes: the country-level results framework report constitutes the attestation record, supplemented by partner government confirmation.

### Continuation Gate

For programmes spanning multiple funding periods:

A continuing ESIF programme must demonstrate: 2021-2027 performance framework milestone achievement as of the mid-term review, evaluation plan execution on schedule, and mandatory common indicator data maintained at the required frequency. A programme that enters the continuation gate with significant milestone underachievement must include a corrective action plan.

A continuing European foundation grantee must demonstrate: prior period outcome indicators achieved or explained, IOOI chain updated to reflect learning from the prior period, and any changes to the commercial revenue architecture (for Sub-type A, D) disclosed with an updated additionality argument.

## Part 2: WALKRI Field Specifications

The following field specifications cover the intake fields most commonly required in European philanthropy and EU structural fund programs. Each specification satisfies WALKRI's five pre-publication requirements: criterion intent, operational definition, response form, evidence form, conformance threshold.

---

### Field: EU Added Value Declaration

**Criterion intent:** Establishes what the EU-level investment produces beyond what member state action alone would achieve, satisfying the additionality requirement of the ESIF regulatory framework and enabling assessment of whether EU funding produces genuinely additional outcomes.

**Operational definition:** EU added value is the contribution of EU intervention above and beyond what could have been achieved by member states alone. It is assessed relative to a counterfactual: the situation that would have prevailed if the EU programme had not existed. EU added value may take several forms: scale (EU funding enables a programme at a scale no single member state could finance), cross-border (EU investment addresses issues that spill across member state boundaries), leverage (EU funding attracts additional public or private co-investment that would not otherwise have been mobilized), or innovation (EU investment supports approaches that member states would not have prioritized under national budget constraints). The counterfactual must be specified; a generic claim of EU value without a named counterfactual does not satisfy this field.

**Response form:** A named form of EU added value from the recognized categories, a description of the counterfactual situation (what would have happened without EU investment), and a statement of what the EU investment produces relative to that counterfactual. For projects within ESIF programmes: the EU added value declaration must align with the programme-level EU added value statement in the programme document.

**Evidence form:** At completion: evidence that the declared form of EU added value was produced. For scale: output volumes that demonstrably exceed what national budgets supported. For cross-border: documented evidence of cross-border cooperation or spillover effects. For leverage: co-investment volumes and confirmation that co-investors would not have participated without the EU anchor investment. For innovation: documentation of the novel approach and evidence of uptake beyond the funded project.

**Conformance threshold:** A named counterfactual and a named form of EU added value with a corresponding evidence plan. An EU added value declaration that does not specify a counterfactual does not satisfy this field. A declaration that identifies EU added value as "contributing to EU priorities" without specifying what would have occurred without EU investment does not satisfy this field.

---

### Field: Mandatory Common Indicator Reference

**Criterion intent:** Identifies which mandatory common output and result indicators from the applicable fund regulation apply to this programme or project, ensuring that the pre-specified indicator requirements of the ESIF regulatory framework are met and that programme data can be aggregated across the full programme and across member states.

**Operational definition:** Mandatory common indicators are output and result indicators codified in the annexes of the relevant fund regulation (CPR 2021/1060 and fund-specific regulations) that must be reported by all Managing Authorities using that fund. They are pre-specified by the regulation; programmes may not substitute alternative indicators for mandatory common indicators, though they may add supplementary programme-specific indicators. For ERDF: indicators in Annex I of Regulation 2021/1058. For ESF+: indicators in Annex I of Regulation 2021/1057. For JTF: indicators in Annex II of Regulation 2021/1056. For the Cohesion Fund: indicators in Annex I of Regulation 2021/1058 (shared with ERDF for the relevant intervention fields).

**Response form:** For each intervention field in the programme or project: the mandatory common indicator code (as specified in the relevant regulatory annex), the indicator name, the unit of measurement, the baseline value, and the target value. For output indicators: the target at programme end. For result indicators: the target value in the relevant reference year. Supplementary programme-specific indicators may be listed separately.

**Evidence form:** Monitoring data collected throughout the programme period at the frequency specified in the performance framework (typically annual for annual performance reports to the European Commission). Participant-level data for ESF+ indicators must be collected at participant entry and exit from the supported operation.

**Conformance threshold:** All mandatory common indicators for the relevant fund and intervention field must be declared with baseline and target values before the programme opens. A programme design that omits a mandatory common indicator applicable to its intervention field does not satisfy this field. Programmes that cannot establish a baseline before activities begin must include a baseline measurement plan with a timeline and must not report results until the baseline is established.

### Field: ESF+ Participant-Level Disaggregation

**Criterion intent:** Establishes the disaggregation categories for participant-level data collection required under ESF+ Regulation 2021/1057, ensuring that participant characteristics can be reported across the full programme and aggregated at the European Commission level.

**Operational definition:** ESF+ mandatory participant disaggregation covers five dimensions collected at participant entry and exit: (1) age (under 30, 30-54, 55 and above); (2) gender (female, male, non-binary where nationally recognized); (3) employment status at entry (employed including self-employed, unemployed, long-term unemployed, inactive, inactive and not in education or training); (4) educational attainment level using ISCED 2011 categories (levels 0-2, levels 3-4, levels 5-8); and (5) household situation (single adult with dependent children, other). Additional disaggregation categories apply for participants with disabilities, migrants, and other target populations depending on the programme's specific objectives. All five dimensions must be declared at the entry gate and collected for all participants; retroactive disaggregation is not permitted.

**Response form:** A data collection plan specifying: which of the five disaggregation dimensions apply to this programme (all five are mandatory for ESF+ operations), the data collection instrument for each dimension, the data collection timing (at entry, at exit, at follow-up), and the data management system that will hold participant-level records. The plan must address data protection compliance with GDPR for personal data collected at the participant level.

**Evidence form:** Participant-level records maintained in the programme's management information system, with disaggregated summary statistics available for the annual performance report. For the European Commission's aggregate reporting: Member State management authority systems must be able to extract disaggregated data in the format specified by the Commission's technical specifications.

**Conformance threshold:** All five disaggregation dimensions must be declared and planned for collection at the entry gate. A data collection plan that defers disaggregation method design until after participant enrollment begins does not satisfy this field. GDPR compliance documentation must be in place before participant data collection begins; collection without GDPR compliance constitutes a regulatory violation, not merely an evidence gap.

### Field: Theory of Change with IOOI Mapping

**Criterion intent:** Establishes the causal chain from programme inputs and activities to outputs, outcomes, and impact using the PHINEO IOOI framework standard in German-language European philanthropy, providing the Theory of Change required by CROSS at the entry gate.

**Operational definition:** The PHINEO IOOI framework is a four-tier measurement structure: Input (resources the programme deploys), Output (direct results of programme activities, typically countable), Outcome (changes in participant conditions attributable to programme activities, typically measurable over time), and Impact (contribution to the broad social goal the programme works toward, shared with other actors and assessed over the long run). This maps directly to CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy: inputs and activities at the base, outputs one tier up, outcomes above that, and impact (or goal) at the top tier. The IOOI chain must show how each tier contributes to the next; a programme that lists indicators at multiple tiers without articulating the causal links between them has not completed the IOOI chain.

**Response form:** An IOOI chain with at least one element at each of the four tiers, stated in the following format: Input (what the programme deploys in resources), Output (what activities produce, stated in units), Outcome (what changes in participant or system conditions, stated in measurable terms with a timeframe), Impact (what the programme contributes to at the societal level, named and linked to the outcome). For German bilateral programmes using BMZ/GIZ frameworks: the IOOI chain must align with the BMZ results model; the BMZ results model adds a module tier below the output tier for administrative completeness.

**Evidence form:** At entry: the IOOI chain as a structured document or programme logic model, approved by the foundation's programme officer or the BMZ/GIZ results measurement team before funds are committed. At completion: outcome measurement results linked to the declared outcome indicators, and an impact assessment or contribution to impact argument linked to the declared impact tier.

**Conformance threshold:** A complete four-tier IOOI chain with causal links between tiers and at least one measurable indicator at the output and outcome tiers. A programme that declares an impact statement ("improving social cohesion") without a corresponding outcome indicator linked to programme activities does not satisfy this field. The impact tier of the IOOI chain is typically shared with other actors and not fully attributable to the programme; programmes must acknowledge this and present a contribution argument rather than an attribution claim at the impact tier.

### Field: Performance Framework Milestone Declaration

**Criterion intent:** Establishes the mid-term milestones and end targets for each output and result indicator in the ESIF performance framework, enabling the European Commission to assess programme progress at the 2025 mid-term performance review and triggering performance reserve allocation or reallocation accordingly.

**Operational definition:** The ESIF performance framework consists of output indicators and result indicators with targets set for the end of the programming period (2029) and milestones set for the mid-term performance review (2025, assessing 2024 achievement). Under CPR 2021/1060 Article 18, the Commission reviews performance against 2024 milestones in 2025 and may reallocate the performance reserve (8% of each fund allocation) away from programmes that significantly underachieve milestones. Milestones are set by the Managing Authority in the programme document; they must reflect realistic expectations of progress based on programme design and implementation capacity. Milestones that are set artificially low to guarantee achievement do not satisfy the spirit of the performance framework and may be adjusted during programme negotiation with the Commission.

**Response form:** For each output and result indicator in the programme: the 2024 milestone value (for the mid-term performance review), the end-of-period target value (2029), the unit of measurement, and the data source and collection frequency. For programmes with multiple priority axes or specific objectives: a performance framework table with indicators, milestones, and targets for each axis/objective. The performance framework table is the standard format; it must be submitted to the European Commission as part of the programme document.

**Evidence form:** Annual performance reports submitted to the European Commission by 31 January each year, covering progress against milestones and targets for the preceding calendar year. The 2025 mid-term performance review uses data from the 2024 annual performance report. Programmes must maintain monitoring data at the project level that can be aggregated to the programme level for the annual performance report.

**Conformance threshold:** Milestones and targets for all output and result indicators must be set before the programme begins operations. Milestones must be set at a level that reflects realistic expectations given programme design and implementation capacity; milestones set at zero or negligibly low values for early years without justification do not satisfy this field. Significant milestone underachievement (defined in CPR 2021/1060 as achievement of less than 65% of the 2024 milestone by the end of 2024) triggers performance review consequences.

### Field: Evaluation Plan Reference

**Criterion intent:** Establishes the CPR Article 45 evaluation plan as the entry specification gate document for ESIF programmes, confirming that Managing Authorities have committed to a forward-looking evaluation agenda before programme activities begin.

**Operational definition:** CPR Article 45 requires each Managing Authority to prepare an evaluation plan at programme start, setting out the evaluations to be conducted during the programming period. An evaluation plan specifies: the evaluations to be carried out, the timing of each evaluation, the questions each evaluation will address, the data sources each evaluation will use, and the budget allocated to evaluation activities. The evaluation plan is the Managing Authority's commitment to systematic learning about programme performance; it must be prepared before the programme opens and must cover the full 2021-2027 period. CROSS's entry specification gate is satisfied for ESIF programmes when the evaluation plan is in place and covers the indicators and impact questions relevant to the programme's obligation mode.

**Response form:** A reference to the Managing Authority's CPR Article 45 evaluation plan: the document title, approval date, the evaluations it schedules, and the questions relevant to this specific programme or project. For project-level applications within an ESIF programme: confirmation that the programme-level evaluation plan covers the intervention type and that project-level monitoring data will be available to programme evaluators.

**Evidence form:** The published CPR Article 45 evaluation plan, accessible from the Managing Authority's website or programme management information system. For project-level applicants: confirmation from the Managing Authority that the programme evaluation plan is in place and that the project's monitoring obligations are aligned with the programme evaluation plan's data requirements.

**Conformance threshold:** A published CPR Article 45 evaluation plan with a date that predates programme operations. A programme that has begun disbursing funds without a published evaluation plan does not satisfy this field. Project-level applicants that cannot confirm the existence of a programme-level evaluation plan must flag this as a gap at the application gate; funding is not blocked by the gap, but the gap must be reported.

### Field: Social Enterprise Revenue Architecture

**Criterion intent:** Establishes the commercial revenue model of a social enterprise applicant and its relationship to the social mission, enabling assessment of financial sustainability and the causal relationship between commercial activity and social outcome production.

**Operational definition:** A social enterprise revenue architecture describes how the organization generates commercial revenue (from market activities, fees for services, product sales, or investment returns) alongside or in service of its social mission. Three structural relationships are possible: integrated (the revenue-generating activity and the social outcome activity are the same; serving disadvantaged clients at market rates produces both revenue and social outcome), complementary (commercial revenue subsidizes a separate social mission activity; a trading arm funds a free service), or hybrid (some clients pay market rates, others are served at subsidized rates, creating a cross-subsidy model). The architecture declaration must name which model applies and how revenue flows support social outcome delivery.

**Response form:** A description of the commercial revenue sources (what the enterprise sells and to whom), the relationship model (integrated, complementary, or hybrid), and the proportion of total revenue coming from commercial sources versus grants and donations. For EVPA-funded organizations: the revenue architecture as disclosed in the EVPA impact plan. For ICMA Social Bond issuers: the use-of-proceeds breakdown as specified in the Social Bond framework.

**Evidence form:** At entry: audited financial statements for the prior year (or financial projections for new enterprises) showing revenue source breakdown. At completion: actual revenue breakdown for the grant period, confirming that commercial revenue architecture operated as declared. For ICMA Social Bond reporting: the annual impact report covering use of proceeds and revenue allocation.

**Conformance threshold:** A named revenue model (integrated, complementary, or hybrid) with a revenue source breakdown. A social enterprise that claims integrated social and commercial activity without specifying which clients or services generate the commercial revenue does not satisfy this field. Projected revenue architecture that differs significantly from actual results must be disclosed mid-grant; material divergence from the declared architecture is a completion gate issue.

## Part 3: Compatibility Map

This section maps European philanthropy program types to the frameworks in the CROSS+WALKRI compatibility corpus that are most directly applicable. The map is organized by primitive.

---

### Pre-specification Primitive

The pre-specification primitive requires that indicators, criteria, and impact metrics be committed to before any applicant submits. European programs that implement this primitive with the highest degree of regulatory codification:

- **ESIF CPR Article 45 evaluation plans**: the most formally regulated pre-specification requirement in any regional grant system; Managing Authorities are legally obligated to produce evaluation plans before programmes open.
- **EVPA five-step model (plan stage)**: Step 1 requires Theory of Change and indicators defined before any grant commitment.
- **PHINEO IOOI framework**: the plan-before-measure principle embedded in the IOOI chain structure; the output and outcome tiers must be defined before activities begin.
- **ESIF mandatory common indicators**: pre-specified by regulatory annex; programmes may not substitute alternative indicators.

Compatibility statements: ESIF CPR 2021/1060 (Articles 18, 45), EVPA Five-Step Impact Measurement, PHINEO Social Impact Navigator, OECD DAC Criteria, 360Giving Data Standard.

---

### EU Added Value as Causality Stance Primitive

The EU added value criterion requires a counterfactual causal argument that is structurally equivalent to CROSS's additionality declaration. This is the most formally institutionalized causality stance requirement in any regional grant framework:

- **ESIF CPR 2021/1060**: EU added value as a required evaluation criterion under the CPR evaluation framework.
- **CROSS additionality argument**: the canonical implementation of this primitive across all program types.
- **Nordic results-based management country-ownership principle**: the equivalent causality argument in bilateral aid contexts; the host country's ownership of results is the additionality criterion for Sida, Norad, and Danida.

Compatibility statements: ESIF CPR 2021/1060, OECD DAC (evaluation criteria including relevance and impact), CROSS Part VI-A (additionality declaration).

---

### Mandatory Indicator Sets as Pre-specification Evidence

The ESIF mandatory common indicator sets are the most formally codified pre-specified indicator sets in any regional grant system. They provide the compatibility anchor for the pre-specification primitive in EU contexts:

- **ERDF Annex I (Regulation 2021/1058)**: mandatory common output and result indicators for ERDF-funded interventions across all 27 member states.
- **ESF+ Annex I (Regulation 2021/1057)**: mandatory common output and result indicators with participant-level disaggregation requirements.
- **JTF Annex II (Regulation 2021/1056)**: mandatory common indicators for Just Transition Fund interventions.
- **CROSS eleven-field indicator specification**: satisfies mandatory indicator documentation as a structural consequence of conformance.

Compatibility statements: ESIF fund-specific regulations (2021/1056, 2021/1057, 2021/1058), CROSS indicator specification framework, 360Giving Data Standard.

---

### Portfolio Aggregation with Financial Consequences

The ESIF performance framework with mid-term financial consequences is the most operationally consequential portfolio aggregation mechanism in the corpus:

- **ESIF performance framework (CPR Article 18)**: milestone achievement at the 2025 mid-term review determines performance reserve allocation for the 2021-2027 period.
- **CROSS portfolio aggregation mechanism**: applied at the funder level across all grant programs; the ESIF performance framework is the closest external parallel.
- **360Giving Data Standard**: portfolio-level data aggregation for UK philanthropy; no financial consequence mechanism, but strong data infrastructure for aggregation.

Compatibility statements: ESIF CPR 2021/1060 (Article 18 performance framework), INDIGO Social Impact Bond database (portfolio data aggregation), CROSS portfolio aggregation mechanism.

---

### Disaggregation Primitive

The ESF+ participant-level mandatory disaggregation across 27 member states is the most formally codified disaggregation requirement in the corpus:

- **ESF+ Regulation 2021/1057 Annex I**: five mandatory disaggregation dimensions (age, gender, employment status, educational attainment, household situation) collected at participant entry and exit.
- **CROSS disaggregation ratchet**: the principle that disaggregation requirements can only increase or hold; once a program has declared disaggregation by a given dimension, it cannot remove that dimension in a subsequent period.
- **OECD DAC gender marker**: the gender disaggregation requirement in DAC-aligned bilateral aid; less granular than ESF+ but broader in geographic scope.

Compatibility statements: ESF+ Regulation 2021/1057, OECD DAC Gender Marker, CROSS disaggregation ratchet, Nordic results-based management frameworks.

---

### Coherence as Theory of Change Primitive

The OECD DAC coherence criterion and its EU ESIF equivalent require that programme activities are mutually reinforcing and do not undermine other interventions. This maps to the internal coherence of the CROSS Theory of Change hierarchy:

- **OECD DAC coherence criterion**: the mutual reinforcement of development interventions; used by Sida, Norad, Danida, and referenced by IsDB.
- **EU ESIF coherence evaluation criterion**: coherence of programme activities with each other and with EU-level strategies; included in the CPR evaluation framework alongside relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, and EU added value.
- **PHINEO IOOI**: coherence is implicit in the causal chain; activities that do not contribute to outputs, outputs that do not contribute to outcomes, or outcomes that do not contribute to impact represent coherence failures.

Compatibility statements: OECD DAC Criteria, ESIF CPR 2021/1060, PHINEO Social Impact Navigator, BMZ/GIZ Results Model.

---

### Social Bond Reporting as Attestation Primitive

ICMA Social Bond Principles define four components of social bond reporting that map to CROSS's attestation gate structure:

- **Use of proceeds**: what the bond funds, declared at issuance (entry specification gate equivalent).
- **Project evaluation and selection**: how projects are selected for funding (application gate equivalent).
- **Management of proceeds**: how funds are tracked and segregated (ongoing monitoring equivalent).
- **Reporting**: annual impact report covering use of proceeds and social outcomes (attestation gate equivalent).

The Social Bond Principles provide the closest parallel to CROSS's four-gate structure in capital markets contexts.

Compatibility statements: ICMA Social Bond Principles, EU GECES Social Impact Measurement (2014), EVPA Five-Step Impact Measurement, INDIGO Social Impact Bond database.
