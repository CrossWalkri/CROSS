# CROSS+WALKRI Program Bundle: Corporate CSR and Social Impact

---

## Overview

This bundle covers grant and funding programs through which corporations distribute community investment resources, whether driven by voluntary strategy, mandatory statutory compliance, or ESG reporting obligations. The ecosystem includes direct corporate foundation grants, shared value / CSV programs that link grant activity to business strategy, ESG-linked social investment portfolios, and corporate-community investment programs reported under sector standards such as LBG/B4SI or under the India CSR mandate (Companies Act 2013, Section 135).

Four program sub-types operate in this space with materially different obligation structures. This bundle addresses all four and notes where provisions diverge.

**Sub-type A: Corporate foundation grants** distribute funds from a company-controlled foundation or community investment budget, often including employee matching programs, community investment funds, and product donation programs. The grant is voluntary but subject to internal governance and may include non-cash contributions that require valuation.

**Sub-type B: Shared value / CSV programs** structure grants around a business case: the grant funds activities that simultaneously produce community outcomes and advance the company's strategic interests (market development, supply chain strengthening, workforce development). Both the community outcome and the business value are declared and measured.

**Sub-type C: ESG-linked social investment** ties grant activity to the company's ESG disclosure obligations. Outcome indicators are selected because they map to ESG reporting frameworks (GRI, SASB, TCFD, CSRD). The grant functions as both a community investment and an ESG data-collection mechanism.

**Sub-type D: Corporate-community investment reporting** covers programs structured to comply with a formal standard or statutory mandate: LBG/B4SI Corporate Community Investment Standard, India CSR (Companies Act 2013, Section 135), or equivalent jurisdiction-specific requirements. The entry specification gate and completion gate must satisfy the standard's measurement requirements.

## Part 1: CROSS Runbook

### Obligation Mode Selection

**Sub-types A and B:** Use Build obligation mode for grants that fund infrastructure, capacity, or a defined deliverable (a community center, a training curriculum, a piece of equipment). Use Change obligation mode for grants designed to shift a measurable community condition (reduced food insecurity rates, increased employment rates in a target geography, improved health outcomes). Most corporate grants blend both: a Build component delivers a physical or organizational asset; a Change component tracks whether the asset produces the intended condition change.

**Sub-type B (shared value / CSV):** Declare both the community obligation mode (Build or Change) and the business value mode. The business value mode uses the same Build/Change structure applied to the corporate strategic objective. Both must be specified at the entry gate.

**Sub-type C (ESG-linked):** Use Change obligation mode for ESG-relevant outcome indicators. The entry gate must identify the ESG framework and the specific disclosure item the grant activity is intended to address. The indicator specification must satisfy both CROSS's completion gate requirements and the disclosure standard's measurement requirements.

**Sub-type D (LBG/B4SI or India CSR mandate):** Use the obligation mode that matches the funded activity. India CSR projects above the mandatory impact assessment threshold (INR 1 crore and 3 years duration) must include a pre-specified impact assessment plan at the entry gate. The impact assessment plan constitutes the completion gate specification for the mandatory third-party assessment.

### Entry Specification Gate

Before any application window opens, or before any grant commitment is made (for internally allocated corporate grants), the following must be committed to in writing and published in a verifiable location (corporate governance record, foundation board minutes, or equivalent):

**Program specification:**
- Obligation mode (Build, Change, or both)
- Total funding pool, currency denomination, and contribution types (cash, employee time at stated hourly rate, in-kind at stated valuation method)
- Eligible program categories and geographic scope
- Eligibility criteria for applying organizations or designated community partners
- Impact metrics that will be used at the completion gate
- For India CSR: mandatory impact assessment plan for any project exceeding INR 1 crore and 3 years duration

**Input valuation methodology:** Corporate programs frequently blend funding types that must be converted to a common unit. The entry gate must specify the valuation method for each contribution type:
- Cash contributions: face value
- Employee volunteer time: either the London Benchmarking Group standard rate or the company's internal hourly cost rate; the method chosen must be stated and applied consistently across the program
- Pro bono professional services: the standard market rate for the service category or the employee's fully loaded compensation rate; stated method applied consistently
- In-kind product donations: retail value, wholesale cost, or fair market value; stated method applied consistently
- Secondments and loaned executives: full compensation cost for the period, or an agreed fractional rate; stated method applied consistently

Non-cash contributions that are not converted to a common unit using a disclosed method cannot be reported as part of the program total.

**Business case documentation (Sub-type B):** Corporate grants in shared value programs require disclosure of the business value the grant is designed to generate alongside the community value. The entry gate must specify: which strategic objective the grant addresses, what business metric will be tracked (market size, supply chain volume, employee recruitment pipeline, brand metric), and what the anticipated relationship is between the community outcome and the business objective. CROSS's coherence disclosure and revenue architecture fields apply to Sub-type B programs.

**Causality stance:** Most corporate programs claim contribution rather than attribution. The entry gate must declare which causality stance applies:
- Attribution (the grant is the primary cause of the stated outcome; independent verification required)
- Contribution (the grant is one among multiple factors contributing to the outcome; contribution pathway must be stated)
- Association (the grant is associated with the stated outcome but causality is not claimed; association rationale must be stated)

Programs claiming attribution without an independent verification mechanism declared at the entry gate cannot satisfy this field at the completion gate.

**Concurrent funding disclosure:** All funding sources contributing to the same scope must be disclosed:
- Other corporate grants or joint-funder programs covering the same geography and target population
- Government grants or contracts funding the same community partner for the same scope
- Foundation grants from third parties funding overlapping activities
- Employee giving programs and individual donations that are counted toward corporate CSR totals must be distinguished from corporate direct grants

**GRI Standards alignment (Sub-types C and D):** Programs seeking GRI 203 or GRI 413 alignment must declare at the entry gate which GRI disclosure items the program is intended to address and what measurement architecture will produce the required data.

### Application Gate

**Organizational identity for grantee partners:** The applying community organization must declare: legal name and registration number, jurisdiction of registration, principal office address and primary service geography, primary contact and authorized signatory, and whether the organization is a majority community-owned, community-controlled, or community-led entity (relevant for Sub-type D participatory programs).

**Activity and output specification:** Each funded activity must specify: a name, a description of what the activity is and what it is not, the target population (demographic and geographic), the unit of measurement for outputs (number of participants, number of training hours, square meters of infrastructure), the target value, and the completion criterion. Outputs must be distinguishable from the grant's intended outcomes.

**Theory of Change declaration:** The application must map the funded activities to anticipated outputs and from outputs to anticipated outcomes, following the LBG/B4SI three-level chain: input, output, impact. Programs seeking GRI or PHINEO IOOI compatibility must extend the chain to include the goal level (the population condition the outcomes contribute to).

**India CSR application requirements (Sub-type D):** Applications for projects requiring mandatory impact assessment must include: a draft impact assessment plan naming the assessment agency (which must be an independent agency registered with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs), the assessment methodology, the timing of the assessment relative to project completion, and the reporting pathway to the company's Board of Directors and Annual Report.

### Completion Gate

**Build obligation:** Completion evidence is the delivered infrastructure or capacity asset. The review must confirm: the asset exists, is operational, is accessible to the declared target population, and was completed within the grant period. For training programs: participant records demonstrating delivery. For physical infrastructure: photographs, inspection records, or equivalent documentary evidence.

**Change obligation:** Completion evidence is indicator data demonstrating movement toward the specified community condition. The data must come from a pre-specified measurement source declared at the entry gate. Outcome claims based on measurement sources not declared at the entry gate do not satisfy the completion gate.

**Input contribution reconciliation:** At completion, the program must reconcile declared non-cash contributions against the valuation method stated at the entry gate. If volunteer hours were declared as a contribution type, the completion record must include hours logged at the stated rate.

**India CSR impact assessment (Sub-type D):** For projects meeting the mandatory threshold, completion is not confirmed until the third-party impact assessment report is received, reviewed by the Board, and filed in the Annual Report. The completion gate for these projects has a two-stage structure: (1) activity completion evidence, and (2) third-party impact assessment report received and filed.

**LBG/B4SI independent verification:** Programs reporting to the LBG/B4SI network may elect independent verification of their measurement data. If independent verification is elected, the verification pathway must be declared at the entry gate; the completion gate is confirmed by the verified data file, not the unverified self-report.

### Attestation Gate and Corpus

The Attestation Corpus for corporate CSR programs should be structured to satisfy both internal governance requirements and any applicable statutory or standards reporting obligations:

- Program specification: recorded in board minutes, CSR committee charter, or equivalent governance document before the program opens
- Application record: stored in the company's grant management system with date stamp
- Completion gate determination: recorded in CSR committee or foundation board minutes; India CSR projects include the third-party impact assessment report as a corpus document
- Disbursement record: payment records by contribution type (cash, time, in-kind), with conversion to common unit applied at the stated valuation methodology
- GRI or LBG/B4SI reporting record: the relevant disclosure document with stable reference to the underlying grant corpus

For India CSR programs, the full corpus must be available for filing in the company's Annual Report and available for inspection by regulatory authorities.

### Continuation Gate

For multi-year programs or programs that renew annually:

A continuing community partner must demonstrate: delivery of all prior year commitments against specified outputs and outcomes, resolution of any outstanding input valuation reconciliation, and confirmation that the organizational identity declarations remain current.

A continuing program operator must demonstrate: the entry specification gate for the new grant cycle is published before the prior cycle closes; the attestation corpus for the prior cycle is complete; and, for India CSR programs, the mandatory impact assessment schedule is on track for any projects meeting the assessment threshold.

## Part 2: WALKRI Field Specifications

The following field specifications cover the intake fields most commonly required in corporate CSR and social impact grant programs. Each specification satisfies WALKRI's five pre-publication requirements: criterion intent, operational definition, response form, evidence form, compliance threshold.

---

### Field: Business Case and Strategic Alignment

**Criterion intent:** Documents the relationship between the grant activity and the corporate funder's strategic objectives, enabling transparent assessment of whether the program is a genuine shared value investment or a grant with a post-hoc business rationale attached. This field is mandatory for Sub-type B (shared value / CSV) programs and recommended for any program that will be reported in a corporate sustainability report.

**Operational definition:** Strategic alignment is the stated relationship between a specific corporate business objective (a named market, a named supply chain function, a named workforce pipeline, a named brand or reputation objective) and the community outcome the grant funds. Business case documentation describes the anticipated causal pathway from the community outcome to the business objective: why achieving the community outcome is expected to advance the corporate strategic interest.

**Response form:** A narrative of 200-400 words covering: the corporate strategic objective by name, the community outcome the grant funds, and the stated pathway between the two. Programs that do not claim a business case (pure philanthropy) may enter "No business case claimed" and provide a rationale for why the grant falls within the company's community investment policy without a strategic alignment rationale.

**Evidence form:** For Sub-type B programs: the relevant section of the company's corporate strategy, annual report, or strategic planning document that names the business objective the grant addresses. For Sub-type C (ESG-linked): the specific GRI, SASB, or CSRD disclosure item the grant activity is intended to address. The evidence form is a citation, not a reproduction of the full document.

**Compliance threshold:** A named corporate strategic objective with a stated causal pathway to the funded community outcome. A generic statement that the grant "aligns with our community investment priorities" without naming a specific strategic objective does not satisfy this field for Sub-type B programs. Sub-type A programs (pure philanthropic grants) satisfy this field with a "No business case claimed" declaration accompanied by a policy rationale.

---

### Field: Community Outcome Indicators

**Criterion intent:** Specifies the measurable community conditions the grant is designed to shift, at sufficient operational precision that independent confirmation of change is possible and that the indicator can be disaggregated by geography and target demographic.

**Operational definition:** A community outcome indicator is a measurable condition in the target population or geography that the grant is designed to change. The indicator must be: (a) stated in terms of the population's condition, not the grantee organization's activity; (b) measurable with a named data source or measurement protocol; (c) disaggregatable by the geographic and demographic categories relevant to the target population; and (d) attributable or contribuable to the grant activity through the stated Theory of Change pathway.

**Response form:** One or more indicators, each specifying: indicator name, unit of measurement, baseline value (or declaration that baseline collection is a grant deliverable), target value, target date, measurement source (named survey, administrative data system, or measurement protocol), and disaggregation dimensions (geography at the appropriate level; demographic dimensions relevant to the program such as age, gender, income, disability status). Programs reporting under LBG/B4SI must map each indicator to the relevant LBG measurement level (input, output, or impact).

**Evidence form:** At completion: data from the specified measurement source, disaggregated at the declared dimensions, covering the grant period. For programs where the baseline was a grant deliverable: the baseline dataset collected during the grant period along with the endline dataset. Data must come from the source declared at the entry gate; data from an alternative source requires a documented explanation of why the declared source was not used.

**Compliance threshold:** Each indicator must have a named measurement source, a baseline or baseline collection plan, and a target value. An indicator without a baseline or a named measurement source cannot be confirmed at the completion gate. Indicators stated only at the organizational output level (number of training sessions delivered) do not satisfy this field as community outcome indicators; they may appear separately as output specifications.

---

### Field: Input Valuation Methodology

**Criterion intent:** Establishes the method by which all non-cash contributions are converted to a monetary unit, ensuring that the total program investment is reported on a consistent and comparable basis and that different contribution types are not conflated or double-counted.

**Operational definition:** Input valuation methodology is the set of rules that assigns a monetary value to each non-cash contribution type the program includes: employee volunteer hours, pro bono professional services, in-kind product donations, loaned executive time, and any other non-cash transfer of value to the community partner. The methodology must be stated before any contributions are made so that contributors know how their time or materials will be valued.

**Response form:** For each contribution type included in the program: the contribution type name, the valuation basis (LBG standard volunteer rate; internal cost rate; market rate for professional services; retail value; wholesale cost; fair market value as defined by which source), the specific rate or value used (dollars per hour for time-based contributions; price per unit for in-kind), and the source of the rate (LBG published rate for the current year; company HR system rate; market survey citation). Programs that include only cash contributions may enter "Cash only; no non-cash valuation required."

**Evidence form:** The published source for each rate used: the LBG/B4SI published benchmark rate document (with version year), the company's internal HR rate schedule (internal document reference acceptable; the rate itself must be stated), or the market rate survey citation. At completion: a contribution log showing each contribution type, quantity (hours or units), rate applied, and total value by type, summed to the program total.

**Compliance threshold:** Each non-cash contribution type must have a named valuation basis, a specific rate, and a source for the rate. A program that includes volunteer hours without a stated hourly rate, or in-kind contributions without a stated valuation basis, does not satisfy this field. The same valuation method must be applied consistently to the same contribution type across the program period; mid-program changes to the valuation method require a documented explanation and may require restatement of prior period contributions.

---

### Field: Employee Engagement Metrics

**Criterion intent:** Specifies the measurement approach for employee-giving programs, skills-based volunteering programs, and other grant activities in which employee participation is a program output or a basis for matching contributions, ensuring that participation figures are measured consistently and that the relationship between employee participation and matching contributions is transparent.

**Operational definition:** Employee engagement in a community investment context is any structured participation by company employees in a grant program's funded activities: direct financial giving (individual donations eligible for company match), volunteer hours contributed to a grantee organization, skills-based or pro bono professional services, and board service or secondments. Engagement metrics are the counts, rates, and characteristics of this participation measured at the program level.

**Response form:** For each employee engagement activity included in the program: activity type (giving, volunteering, skills-based volunteering, board service, secondment), participation unit (number of employees, number of hours, number of volunteer events), measurement method (payroll deduction records for giving programs; self-reported timesheets verified by grantee for volunteering; project deliverables for skills-based volunteering), target participation level (number of employees or hours per program period), and matching formula if applicable (for employee-giving programs: the company match rate, the annual per-employee cap, and the eligible recipient categories).

**Evidence form:** At completion: participation records by activity type and unit. For giving programs: payroll deduction totals by period with match calculations. For volunteering: hours logs submitted by employees and confirmed by grantee organizations or volunteer program coordinators. For skills-based volunteering: project completion records with hours documented. Aggregate participation figures without individual or event-level records do not satisfy this field for programs in which employee participation is a basis for matching contributions.

**Compliance threshold:** Each declared employee engagement activity must have a named measurement method and a basis for confirming participation at completion. Participation figures based entirely on self-report without any confirmation mechanism do not satisfy this field. For matching programs: the match calculation must be auditable from payroll records through to disbursement.

### Field: Impact Assessment Plan

**Criterion intent:** For India CSR programs meeting the mandatory impact assessment threshold (INR 1 crore project cost and 3 years duration), establishes the pre-specified plan for third-party impact assessment that is required by the Companies Act 2013 Section 135 and the accompanying CSR Rules. For programs below this threshold, or in other jurisdictions, establishes the evaluation plan when independent assessment is elected.

**Operational definition:** An impact assessment plan is a pre-specified document naming: the assessment agency, the assessment methodology, the indicators that will be assessed, the timeline for assessment relative to project completion, and the reporting pathway. For India CSR mandatory assessments: the assessment agency must be an independent agency listed with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, and the assessment must be completed within 6 months of project completion. The impact assessment plan is a completion gate pre-condition: it must exist before the entry gate is confirmed.

**Response form:** Agency name and MCA registration status (for India CSR mandatory assessments). Assessment methodology (stated at the level of data collection methods, comparison group or counterfactual approach, and indicator framework to be applied). List of indicators to be assessed (which must be a subset of the indicators declared in the Community Outcome Indicators field). Assessment timeline: planned start date relative to project completion, planned report date. Reporting pathway: how the report will reach the Board of Directors and how it will be reflected in the Annual Report.

**Evidence form:** At entry gate: the impact assessment plan document or the relevant section of the project proposal. At completion gate: the final third-party impact assessment report, the Board resolution noting receipt of the report, and the Annual Report section citing the assessment. For programs above the INR 1 crore / 3-year threshold, the completion gate is not confirmed until the Board resolution is on record.

**Compliance threshold:** A named assessment agency with confirmed MCA registration (for India CSR mandatory programs). An assessment methodology stated at sufficient specificity that the funder can confirm the agency is using the declared approach. An assessment timeline that places the report receipt before the completion gate is finalized. Programs above the mandatory threshold that reach completion without a filed impact assessment report have not satisfied the India CSR completion gate regardless of the quality of their activity-level evidence.

### Field: ESG Reporting Linkage

**Criterion intent:** For Sub-type C programs (ESG-linked social investment), establishes which ESG framework and specific disclosure item the grant activity is designed to address, so that the grant's measurement architecture produces data that can be used directly in the company's ESG disclosure without additional translation.

**Operational definition:** ESG reporting linkage is the stated connection between a grant program's outcome indicators and a specific disclosure item in a named ESG framework. The connection is stated at the field level: which framework, which standard or topic, which disclosure item, and what the grant program contributes to that item's required data. Linkage is not a general statement that the program "supports our ESG goals"; it is a field-level mapping between grant indicators and disclosure line items.

**Response form:** For each ESG framework linkage claimed: framework name (GRI, SASB, TCFD, EU CSRD, ISO 26000, or equivalent), standard or topic identifier (e.g., GRI 413: Local Communities; GRI 203: Indirect Economic Impacts), specific disclosure item within that standard, the grant outcome indicator that provides data for that item, and the data format required by the disclosure item. Programs with no ESG reporting linkage may enter "No ESG reporting linkage; program reported under LBG/B4SI only" or equivalent.

**Evidence form:** The relevant section of the ESG framework's disclosure requirements (by citation, not reproduction). At completion: the company's ESG disclosure showing the grant program's contribution to the stated disclosure item, with a reference back to the grant's completion gate record. The linkage is confirmed when the disclosure item cites data produced by the grant program's measurement system.

**Compliance threshold:** A named framework, a named disclosure item, and a named grant outcome indicator that provides data for the item. A general statement that the program "contributes to our GRI reporting" without a specific disclosure item does not satisfy this field. The grant's outcome indicators must be structured to produce data in the format required by the ESG framework's disclosure item; indicators that require significant post-hoc transformation to fit a disclosure format indicate a misalignment between the grant measurement design and the stated ESG linkage.

### Field: Third-Party Verification Plan

**Criterion intent:** Establishes who will independently confirm the grant program's claims at the completion gate and by what method, distinguishing between mandatory independent assessment (India CSR) and elected independent verification (LBG/B4SI pathway, ESG assurance).

**Operational definition:** Third-party verification is independent confirmation of grant program claims by an entity that is not the corporate funder, not the grantee community organization, and not an employee or agent of either. Independent verification confirms that the declared activities occurred, that the inputs were valued using the declared methodology, and that the outcome indicators reflect actual community conditions rather than self-reported assertions. Verification differs from impact assessment: verification confirms the accuracy of claimed data; assessment evaluates whether the program achieved its intended outcomes.

**Response form:** Verification type (mandatory under India CSR; elected under LBG/B4SI; ESG assurance under ISAE 3000 or equivalent; no independent verification elected). For elected verification: the verifying entity's name and relevant accreditation or qualification. The verification scope: which elements of the program's completion gate record will be verified (all inputs, all outputs, selected outcome indicators, or full program). The verification timing: when the verifier will conduct fieldwork relative to the program completion date. For India CSR mandatory assessment: this field may reference the Impact Assessment Plan field and state the assessment agency as the verification entity.

**Evidence form:** For elected LBG/B4SI verification: the verifier's signed verification statement attached to the program's completion record. For ESG assurance: the auditor's assurance report covering the grant program's contribution to the stated disclosure items. For India CSR: the third-party impact assessment report serves as the verification record; this field references the Impact Assessment Plan field.

**Compliance threshold:** Programs that declare elected verification must name the verifying entity and the verification scope before the entry gate is confirmed. Programs that claim LBG/B4SI independent verification pathway without a named verifier do not satisfy this field. Programs for which verification is mandatory under India CSR must satisfy this field through the Impact Assessment Plan field; the two fields are treated as a linked pair for India CSR programs above the threshold.

### Field: Concurrent Business Value Documentation

**Criterion intent:** For Sub-type B (shared value / CSV) programs, documents the business value generated alongside the community value, ensuring transparency about the dual-purpose nature of the grant and enabling the funder to report both values without conflating them.

**Operational definition:** Concurrent business value is the measurable benefit the corporate funder derives from the grant activity, expressed in terms of the stated corporate strategic objective: market access, supply chain strengthening, workforce pipeline, brand or reputation metric, or equivalent. Concurrent business value documentation is the record of how this benefit was measured, what value was realized, and how it relates to the community value produced by the same activity.

**Response form:** Business value type (market development, supply chain, workforce, brand, or other with description). Business metric used to measure the value (named metric with units and measurement source). Baseline value of the metric before the grant period. Realized or projected value at grant completion. Stated relationship between the community outcome and the business value: whether the community outcome is a means to the business value, a co-benefit of the same activity, or an independently valuable outcome that happens to also advance business interests. For programs in which the business value exceeds the community investment, the ratio must be disclosed.

**Evidence form:** Business metric data from a named internal or external measurement source, covering the baseline and end-of-grant periods. For market development claims: market size or revenue data from a named industry source or the company's internal sales data (aggregated, not individual transaction-level). For supply chain claims: supplier volume or quality data from the company's procurement system. For workforce claims: recruitment or retention data from HR systems. Internal data sources are acceptable for this field; the measurement source and reporting period must be named.

**Compliance threshold:** A named business metric with a baseline value and a measured or projected end value. A narrative that describes business value without a named metric or baseline does not satisfy this field for Sub-type B programs. The community value and business value must be reported separately and must not be summed into a single combined figure that obscures the composition of each.

## Part 3: Compatibility Map

This section maps the corporate CSR and social impact program type to the frameworks in the CROSS+WALKRI compatibility corpus that are most directly applicable. The map is organized by primitive.

---

### Pre-specification Primitive

The pre-specification primitive requires that indicators, criteria, and impact metrics be committed to before any applicant submits or any grant commitment is made. Corporate CSR frameworks that implement this primitive:

- **LBG/B4SI Corporate Community Investment Standard**: three measurement levels (input, output, impact) declared before the program period begins; the standard's input taxonomy requires upfront declaration of contribution types and valuation methods
- **India CSR (Companies Act 2013, Section 135)**: the mandatory impact assessment plan is required at project initiation for projects above the INR 1 crore / 3-year threshold; CROSS's entry specification gate directly addresses this pre-condition
- **PHINEO IOOI**: the IOOI framework (Input, Output, Outcome, Impact) requires the full causal chain to be specified before the program period begins
- **IMP Five Dimensions**: the five dimensions of impact (what, who, how much, contribution, risk) are pre-specified in the grant design, not assessed retrospectively

Compatibility statements: LBG/B4SI, India-CSR-Section-135, PHINEO-IOOI, IMP-Five-Dimensions.

---

### Causality Stance Primitive

The causality stance primitive requires an explicit declaration of whether the program claims attribution, contribution, or association for its stated outcomes. Corporate CSR frameworks that address this primitive:

- **LBG/B4SI impact measurement**: LBG distinguishes between output (what the program produced) and impact (change in community conditions), and acknowledges that contribution rather than attribution is the typical claim for community investment programs
- **GRI 413 (Local Communities)**: GRI 413 requires disclosure of actual and potential impacts on local communities but does not mandate a specific causality standard; CROSS's causality stance declaration provides the missing specification
- **EVPA Impact Measurement**: EVPA's spectrum of evidence explicitly addresses the contribution vs. attribution distinction and provides a ladder of causality claim strength

Compatibility statements: LBG/B4SI, GRI-413, EVPA-Impact-Measurement.

---

### Independent Verification Primitive

The independent verification primitive requires pre-specification of who will independently confirm program claims and by what method. Corporate CSR frameworks that implement this primitive:

- **India CSR third-party impact assessment mandate**: for projects above the INR 1 crore / 3-year threshold, independent verification by an MCA-registered agency is mandatory and must be planned at project initiation
- **LBG/B4SI independent verification pathway**: programs may elect independent verification of their measurement data; the verification scope and timing must be declared before the program period begins
- **ESG assurance (ISAE 3000)**: external assurance of ESG disclosures that include community investment data applies the ISAE 3000 limited or reasonable assurance standard
- **ISO/UNDP 53001**: social responsibility management systems standard that includes third-party verification requirements for reported social impact claims

Compatibility statements: India-CSR-Section-135, LBG/B4SI, ISAE-3000, ISO-UNDP-53001.

---

### Theory of Change Primitive

The Theory of Change primitive requires an explicit mapping from funded activities to outputs to outcomes to goals. Corporate CSR frameworks that implement this primitive:

- **LBG/B4SI three-level measurement chain**: the standard's input-output-impact chain is a simplified Theory of Change requiring explicit mapping at three levels
- **Kellogg Logic Model**: the Logic Model is widely used in corporate foundation grantmaking; CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy maps to its resources-activities-outputs-outcomes-impact chain
- **PHINEO IOOI**: IOOI extends the LBG chain to four levels and requires explicit causal narrative at each level transition
- **Annie E. Casey / Results-Based Accountability (RBA)**: RBA distinguishes population accountability (community condition change) from performance accountability (program output delivery), which maps directly to CROSS's Change vs. Build obligation mode distinction

Compatibility statements: LBG/B4SI, Kellogg-Logic-Model, PHINEO-IOOI, Annie-E-Casey-RBA.

---

### Organizational Identity Primitive

The organizational identity primitive requires declaration of the applying entity's legal identity, governance structure, and accountability relationships. Corporate CSR frameworks that address this primitive:

- **India CSR Schedule VII categories**: the mandatory CSR schedule defines eligible activity categories that also implicitly constrain eligible organizational partners; grantee organizations must fall within the declared category to satisfy India CSR compliance
- **GFGP ARS 1651**: the African continent's grant management standard for grantee capacity assessment; relevant for corporate CSR programs operating in African markets where GFGP compliance is expected
- **GRI 413**: GRI 413 requires disclosure of the processes used to identify, assess, and address community impacts; the grantee community organization's identity is a relevant disclosure element

Compatibility statements: India-CSR-Schedule-VII, GFGP-ARS-1651, GRI-413.

---

### Portfolio Aggregation Primitive

The portfolio aggregation primitive applies when a funder aggregates individual grant outcomes into a portfolio-level report. Corporate CSR frameworks that implement this primitive:

- **LBG/B4SI network-level benchmarking**: LBG aggregates member companies' investment data across the network, enabling cross-company benchmarking of input levels, output types, and impact measurement approaches; CROSS's portfolio aggregation primitive covers how individual grant records are structured to enable this aggregation
- **EU GECES Social Impact Measurement**: the European Commission's social impact measurement framework for the social economy includes portfolio-level aggregation requirements for programs funded through EU structural funds

Compatibility statements: LBG/B4SI, EU-GECES.

---

### Disaggregation Ratchet Primitive

The disaggregation ratchet primitive requires that disaggregated outcome data be reported once collected, and that the disaggregation level cannot be reduced in subsequent reporting periods without disclosure. Corporate CSR frameworks that address this primitive:

- **NCRP racial equity criteria** (referenced in the Participatory and Equity bundle): disaggregation by race and geography is a core criterion; corporate programs serving communities of color that adopt NCRP criteria must report at the race-disaggregated level
- **IFC OPIM (Operational Performance and Impact Metrics)**: IFC's system for investee companies requires disaggregation of beneficiary data by gender and vulnerability status; corporate programs operating in IFC-financed contexts inherit this requirement
- **Equitable Evaluation Initiative**: pre-specifies the disaggregation dimensions for programs seeking equity alignment; CROSS's disaggregation ratchet operates at the statutory level for race-disaggregated data in programs adopting EEI principles

Compatibility statements: NCRP-Racial-Equity-Criteria, IFC-OPIM, Equitable-Evaluation-Initiative.
